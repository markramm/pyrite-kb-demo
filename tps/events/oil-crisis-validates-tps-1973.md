The 1973 OPEC oil embargo and resulting economic shock provided the definitive real-world validation of TPS. While Western automakers — burdened with massive inventories, inflexible production lines, and gas-guzzling product lineups — suffered prolonged losses, [[toyota-motor-corporation]] recovered faster than any competitor. TPS's lean inventory ([[just-in-time]]), flexible production ([[heijunka]], [[smed]]), and built-in quality ([[jidoka]]) proved to be exactly the capabilities needed in a volatile, resource-constrained environment. Japanese competitors took notice: the oil crisis made other Japanese manufacturers study and adopt TPS methods, beginning TPS's spread beyond Toyota. This event transformed TPS from an internal Toyota experiment into a proven competitive advantage, setting the stage for the [[western-discovery-and-lean-1980-present|Western discovery]] that would follow.
