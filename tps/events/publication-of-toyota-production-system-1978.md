[[taiichi-ohno]] publishes [[toyota-production-system-beyond-large-scale-production]] through Diamond, Inc. — the first comprehensive written account of TPS by its creator. Until this publication, TPS knowledge existed primarily as oral tradition transmitted through [[gemba]] practice at [[toyota-motor-corporation]]. The book's publication was significant but its immediate impact was limited to Japan. The English translation (Productivity Press, 1988) would bring Ohno's thinking to Western audiences a decade later, after [[the-machine-that-changed-the-world]] had already created demand for understanding TPS. Ohno's writing style — direct, concrete, allergic to abstraction — meant that many Western readers found the book less accessible than the later codifications by [[james-womack|Womack]], [[daniel-jones|Jones]], and [[jeffrey-liker|Liker]].
