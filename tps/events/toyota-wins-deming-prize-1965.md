[[toyota-motor-corporation]] wins the Deming Prize, administered by [[juse]], recognizing outstanding quality management. This event validated TPS as a world-class system and marked the culmination of the [[postwar-tps-foundations-1950-1965|postwar development period]]. Preparing for the Deming Prize required Toyota to formalize and document its quality systems — a process that helped systematize TPS practices across the entire company. The prize connected Toyota directly to [[w-edwards-deming]]'s legacy: the statistical quality methods Deming taught in 1950 had, in fifteen years, been absorbed, extended, and operationalized into a complete production system that went far beyond anything Deming himself had envisioned.
