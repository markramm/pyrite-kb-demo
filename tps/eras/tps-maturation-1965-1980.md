TPS matures from a production method to a complete management system. [[taiichi-ohno]] extends TPS across all of [[toyota-motor-corporation]]'s operations and into the supplier network ([[denso]] and others). Techniques are refined: [[heijunka]], [[standard-work]], and [[a3-thinking]] are systematized. The [[oil-crisis-validates-tps-1973|1973 oil crisis]] is the pivotal event: while Western automakers stagger under fuel price shocks and inventory costs, Toyota's lean system recovers faster than any competitor, demonstrating TPS's resilience. Japanese competitors begin studying and copying Toyota's methods. [[taiichi-ohno]] publishes [[toyota-production-system-beyond-large-scale-production]] in 1978 and [[workplace-management]] in 1982. [[shigeo-shingo]] publishes his major works. By 1980, TPS is fully developed — the question becomes transmission.
