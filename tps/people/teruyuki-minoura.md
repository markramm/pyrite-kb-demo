Teruyuki Minoura, Toyota managing director who learned TPS directly from [[taiichi-ohno]] and provided some of the most insightful published accounts of TPS philosophy. His [[minoura-thinking-production-system|"Thinking Production System" speech]] (2003) is one of the most valued TPS practitioner accounts because it conveys the spirit of TPS — not just the techniques but the thinking behind them — from someone who experienced Ohno's teaching firsthand. Minoura emphasizes that TPS is fundamentally about developing people's problem-solving capability, not about implementing tools. His description of Ohno's teaching method — asking questions rather than providing answers, forcing managers to go to the [[gemba]] and observe, never accepting an answer without evidence — captures the pedagogical philosophy that made TPS self-sustaining at Toyota.
