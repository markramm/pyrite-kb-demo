Sam Williams is a journalist and technology writer who authored [[free-as-in-freedom-williams]] (2002), the first full-length biography of [[stallman]]. The book documents Stallman's childhood, his years at the [[mit-ai-lab]] during [[mit-ai-lab-hacker-culture-1971-1984]], and the founding of the [[gnu-project]] and [[free-software-foundation]].

Williams conducted extensive interviews with Stallman and his associates to produce an account that is both sympathetic and candid about Stallman's difficult personal style. The book captures the [[hacker-ethic-mit]] environment that shaped Stallman and traces the development of the [[four-freedoms]] philosophy and the [[copyleft]] mechanism.

The book was originally published by O'Reilly Media and later released under a free license. Stallman subsequently produced a revised edition — [[free-as-in-freedom-revised]] — updating and correcting portions of the narrative from his own perspective.

Williams's biography is the primary narrative source on Stallman's personal history and motivation, complementing the philosophical works collected in [[free-software-free-society]]. It is frequently cited alongside [[hackers-levy]] by [[steven-levy]] as essential background for understanding the [[founding-gnu-and-fsf-1983-1991]] era.
