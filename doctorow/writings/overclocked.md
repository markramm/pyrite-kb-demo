*Overclocked: Stories of the Future Present* (2007) is Doctorow's first major short story collection, published by Thunder's Mouth Press. The subtitle — "Stories of the Future Present" — articulates the method: these are not far-future extrapolations but explorations of tendencies already present in contemporary technology and society, pushed one or two steps further to make their implications visible.

## The Collection as Political Fiction

*Overclocked* appeared in the same period as Doctorow's essay work collected in [[content-essays]], and the two volumes should be read together as different modes of making the same arguments. Where [[content-essays]] argues directly about copyright, DRM, and digital rights, the stories in *Overclocked* dramatize those arguments: they show what [[digital-rights-management-critique]] looks like from the inside of a life constrained by locked technology, or what labor exploitation through digital platforms feels like at the level of individual experience.

## Key Stories

**"When Sysadmins Ruled the Earth"** — the collection's most widely anthologized piece — depicts a global catastrophe from the perspective of the technical workers who keep critical infrastructure running. It is a meditation on competence, duty, and the question of what obligations technical people have to broader society. The story resonated particularly in the security and systems administration community and has been reprinted frequently.

**"I, Robot"** reimagines Asimov's robot stories in a surveillance-state context, exploring what it would mean to live in a society where compliance with authority is literally programmed into AI systems, and what resistance to such a system would require.

**"Anda's Game"** engages gold farming — the practice of paying low-wage workers in developing countries to play online games and harvest virtual currency for resale — as a labor story, following a young gamer who discovers the human cost behind the in-game economy she had experienced as pure play. It is one of Doctorow's most direct engagements with global labor exploitation and connects to themes he would develop more fully in [[for-the-win]].

## Significance in Doctorow's Development

*Overclocked* represents Doctorow's short fiction at its most politically focused. The stories are less interested in world-building for its own sake than in using speculative premises to isolate and examine specific political questions. This approach anticipates the tighter, more polemical fiction of [[radicalized]] a decade later.

The collection established Doctorow as a serious short fiction writer beyond the novel form and demonstrated the range of modes — thriller, labor narrative, philosophical fable, domestic realism — in which he could operate. It also continued the [[creative-commons-licensing]] practice of making the texts freely available, with most stories released online before or alongside the print collection.
