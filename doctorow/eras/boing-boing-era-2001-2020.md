The Boing Boing era spans Doctorow's nearly two decades as co-editor of one of the internet's most influential blogs, overlapping with and providing the media infrastructure for all of his other work during the same period. Unlike the other eras in this knowledge base, this one is defined by platform and medium rather than intellectual theme — it describes the context in which Doctorow developed and distributed his ideas.

When Doctorow joined [[boing-boing]] in 2001, blogging was emerging as the internet's dominant discourse form, and Boing Boing was at its center. The site's combination of technology, counterculture, science fiction, and civil liberties advocacy matched Doctorow's sensibility exactly. His posts reached audiences that his books alone could not, and the blog format — short, linkable, conversational — suited the way he thinks: connecting disparate dots into patterns.

Throughout the [[early-blogging-and-cc-era-2000-2007]] and [[eff-and-fiction-era-2007-2015]], Boing Boing was where Doctorow's advocacy found its largest audience. His coverage of [[digital-rights-management-critique]] issues, [[creative-commons-licensing]] adoption, and [[electronic-frontier-foundation]] campaigns helped build a tech-literate public that understood these issues as matters of political economy rather than arcane legal detail.

The platform's decline as a cultural force during the [[chokepoint-and-monopoly-era-2016-2022]] — as social media displaced blogs and algorithmic feeds replaced direct subscriptions — is itself an illustration of [[platform-decay-cycle]] dynamics. Doctorow's launch of [[pluralistic-net]] in 2020 represented a conscious effort to build more durable, platform-independent infrastructure for his work, drawing lessons from what had happened to Boing Boing and to blogging generally.
