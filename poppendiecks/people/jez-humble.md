Jez Humble co-authored "Continuous Delivery" (2010) with Dave Farley and later "Accelerate" (2018) with Nicole Forsgren and [[gene-kim]]. His work on continuous delivery operationalized several Poppendieck principles: [[deliver-as-fast-as-possible]] through deployment pipelines, [[build-integrity-in]] through automated testing, and [[pull-systems-in-software]] through deployment-on-demand. The Poppendiecks' argument that small batches and fast feedback loops amplify learning found its most concrete infrastructure expression in the continuous delivery pipeline — an automated system that makes deployment a routine, low-risk event rather than a ceremony. [[leading-lean-software-development-2009]]'s organizational themes directly prefigure what Humble, Forsgren, and Kim would measure empirically in the DORA metrics research.
