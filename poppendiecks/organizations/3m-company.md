3M (Minnesota Mining and Manufacturing) is the company where [[mary-poppendieck]] spent a significant portion of her career managing IT product development. Its importance to the Poppendieck story is straightforward: it was the site of the cross-domain experience — lean manufacturing on the factory floor, software team management in the IT function — that gave Mary the raw material for translating TPS principles into software terms.

## Lean at 3M

3M was an early American adopter of lean manufacturing practices. Exposure to lean as a live operational system, rather than as a concept studied from books, is what distinguishes Mary's background from most Agile movement founders. She absorbed the logic of [[eliminate-waste]], pull systems, and value stream thinking in a context where they were implemented in physical production — which later gave her the depth to produce the [[seven-wastes-of-software]] translation with genuine fidelity to the source material.

## Significance

3M's role in the Poppendieck canon is primarily biographical: it explains why [[mary-poppendieck]] could credibly claim to be translating lean rather than merely borrowing its vocabulary. The company itself is not a subject of the Poppendieck framework; it is the origin point of the practitioner knowledge the framework is built from.
