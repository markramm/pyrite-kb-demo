Pegasus Communications was a publishing and convening organization co-founded by [[daniel-kim]] that became the primary dissemination infrastructure for systems thinking tools and frameworks in the practitioner community during the 1990s. Its flagship product was "The Systems Thinker" newsletter, a monthly publication that translated research findings, practitioner experiences, and conceptual developments in [[systems-thinking-fifth-discipline]] and the [[learning-organization]] tradition into accessible format for managers, consultants, and organizational developers. The newsletter functioned as a continuing education resource for the community that [[fifth-discipline-1990]] had catalyzed, keeping practitioners current on new applications of [[systems-archetypes]], [[causal-loop-diagrams]], and related tools between the major Senge fieldbook publications.

In addition to "The Systems Thinker," Pegasus hosted the annual Systems Thinking in Action conference, which provided the practitioner community with a regular gathering point for sharing case studies, tools, and methods. The conference was a significant complement to the [[society-for-organizational-learning]]'s more research-oriented gatherings, reaching a broader audience of practitioners who were applying systems thinking in their organizations but who were not necessarily connected to the MIT-anchored academic research community. Pegasus also published standalone resources — [[systems-archetypes]] reference cards, workshop guides, and anthologies of Systems Thinker articles — that became standard practitioner toolkit materials.

Pegasus Communications represents the dissemination infrastructure that translated the conceptual framework of [[fifth-discipline-1990]] into a living practitioner tradition. Without a publication and convening organization dedicated to the ongoing development and sharing of systems thinking practice, the learning organization movement risked remaining confined to the organizations directly connected to [[mit-center-for-organizational-learning]] and [[society-for-organizational-learning]]. Pegasus extended the community's reach significantly, serving readers and conference participants who encountered [[systems-thinking-fifth-discipline]] not through Senge's books directly but through the newsletter, the conference, and the practical tools that Pegasus published and distributed.
