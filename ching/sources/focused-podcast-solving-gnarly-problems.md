"Focused #214: Solving Gnarly Problems, with Clarke Ching" is an 80-minute episode of the Focused podcast hosted by David Sparks and Mike Schmitz on the Relay FM network. Ching discusses finding solutions and fixing systems, both personally and professionally.

## Audience significance

The Focused podcast reaches a mainstream productivity audience -- professionals interested in focus, time management, and personal effectiveness -- rather than the Agile or TOC communities where Ching typically appears. Sparks and Schmitz's audience includes lawyers, knowledge workers, and creative professionals who may never have heard of [[eliyahu-goldratt]] or Theory of Constraints.

This makes the episode one of Ching's highest-reach appearances outside the software community. The "gnarly problems" framing connects directly to [[corkscrew-solutions]] and [[upthinking]] -- the idea that apparently impossible dilemmas can be dissolved by questioning hidden assumptions. Presenting this framework to a general productivity audience tests whether Ching's TOC-derived thinking tools are genuinely domain-agnostic, as he has increasingly claimed during the [[bottleneck-guy-brand-period]].

## Context

The episode appeared in October 2024, the same year as [[the-bottleneck-detective]]. By this point Ching had been expanding his audience beyond software practitioners for several years, through [[cash-cows-make-the-best-burgers]] (CFOs), the detective novel (general business readers), and podcast appearances across non-technical communities. The Focused episode represents the furthest reach of that expansion -- pure productivity audience, no Agile or software framing required.
