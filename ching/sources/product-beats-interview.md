"Episode 64: The Bottleneck Rules" on the Product Beats podcast is an interview in which Ching discusses [[the-bottleneck-rules]] and the [[focccus-formula]]. Product Beats addresses product management and product leadership audiences — a slightly different community than the developer-focused Agile podcast circuit.

## Audience significance

Product management as a discipline sits at the intersection of business priorities and software delivery — exactly the position from which the constraint in a software system is often most visible. A product manager controls the backlog, negotiates scope, and sets priorities; in TOC terms, the product manager's decisions often determine whether the delivery system's constraint is being exploited or overloaded. Ching's appearance on a product management podcast signals that the [[focccus-formula]] and [[bottleneck-thinking]] framework have applications beyond the software delivery team itself.

[[the-bottleneck-rules]] is designed as a short, practical guide rather than a full business novel — a format suited to the podcast-companion reading that many practitioners use. The Product Beats discussion likely walked through the core rules and the FOCCCUS sequence, giving product leaders a framework for thinking about where to focus their attention in a software delivery system.

## Cross-community reach

Alongside the [[agile-uprising-toc-aissance-interview]] and the [[tameflow-campfire-talk]], the Product Beats episode shows Ching reaching across three distinct communities in roughly the same period: mainstream Agile practitioners, TOC-Agile specialists, and product management practitioners. This cross-community presence reflects the genuine applicability of constraint thinking across roles in software organizations — and Ching's ability to frame the same ideas for different audiences without losing the core insight.
