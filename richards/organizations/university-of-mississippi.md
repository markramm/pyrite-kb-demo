## Overview

The University of Mississippi (Ole Miss) is where Richards earned his PhD in mathematics. The institution appears in Richards' intellectual biography primarily as the source of the mathematical training that shaped his analytical approach to strategy and directly precipitated his entry into [[john-boyd]]'s inner circle.

## Significance

Richards' mathematical background was the proximate cause of the Boyd-Richards collaboration. Boyd, preparing "Destruction and Creation" — his foundational paper drawing on Gödel's incompleteness theorem, Heisenberg's uncertainty principle, and the second law of thermodynamics — needed someone to review the mathematical portions for rigor. He approached Richards for this review. The review became a collaboration; the collaboration drew Richards into the [[boyd-circle-period]].

The mathematical formation also shaped how Richards approached Boyd's ideas. Where others in the Boyd circle came from military operations, journalism, or policy backgrounds, Richards brought a technical analytical lens. This is evident in his treatment of [[ooda-based-competition]] — he attends carefully to the structure of the loop and resists the oversimplifications that have proliferated in popular uses of the OODA framework, as his [[boyds-real-ooda-loop]] paper documents.

## Context

Richards' career trajectory after the University of Mississippi — Pentagon from 1971, then Northrop, CACI, and Lockheed — placed his mathematical capabilities in defense-industrial contexts where quantitative analysis of systems performance was central to the work. His Air Force Colonel rank reflects that this was military career, not merely civilian contractor work. The mathematical PhD and the military career together constitute the technical credibility that undergirds his strategic analysis.
