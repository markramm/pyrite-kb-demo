## Overview

Wardley's decision to release his mapping framework under Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 (CC BY-SA 4.0) was a deliberate strategic and philosophical choice that shaped the framework's dissemination and community development.

## The Decision

Rather than commercializing the framework through consulting, certification programs, or proprietary tools — the standard approach for business strategy frameworks — Wardley chose to make his work freely available. Anyone can use, teach, modify, and share Wardley Maps and the associated framework, provided they give attribution and share derivatives under the same license.

## Rationale

Wardley has explained this decision through the lens of his own framework: knowledge about strategy is a component that benefits from commoditization. Keeping it proprietary would create artificial scarcity that slows adoption and learning. Making it open accelerates the evolution of the practice and creates a larger, more diverse community of practitioners who extend the framework in ways a single individual or consultancy could not.

## Impact

The CC licensing has enabled:
- Community-developed tools (OnlineWardleyMaps, etc.)
- Community translations of the book into multiple languages
- Free workshops and teaching materials
- A culture of open sharing within the mapping community
- Academic use without licensing barriers

## Significance

The open licensing decision is significant both practically (it enabled the community) and philosophically (it embodies the doctrine principle that some components should be treated as commodities/utilities rather than proprietary products). It also distinguishes Wardley Mapping from most competing strategy frameworks, which are typically commercialized through consulting firms and certification programs.
