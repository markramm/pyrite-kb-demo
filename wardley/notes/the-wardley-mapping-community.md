## Overview

The Wardley Mapping community is an active, decentralized network of practitioners who use, teach, and extend the mapping framework. The community's existence and character are shaped by Wardley's decision to release his work under Creative Commons (CC BY-SA 4.0), which explicitly invited community ownership of the framework.

## Open-Source Tools

The community has developed several tools for creating and sharing Wardley Maps, enabled by Wardley's [[creative-commons-release]] decision:

**OnlineWardleyMaps (OWM)**: A web-based tool for creating Wardley Maps, developed as an open-source project. It allows users to define maps using a text-based markup language and renders them visually.

**MapScript**: Various community-developed formats and tools for expressing maps in text that can be rendered visually.

**Other tools**: The community has built plugins for various platforms, integration tools, and extensions that expand the mapping ecosystem.

## Community Activities

- **[[map-camp]]**: Community conferences focused on Wardley Mapping practice
- **Online communities**: Active discussion forums, Slack/Discord groups, and social media communities
- **Blog posts and case studies**: Practitioners regularly publish mapping examples and analyses, including [[bits-or-pieces-blog|Wardley's own blog]]
- **Translations**: Community volunteers have translated Wardley's book into multiple languages
- **Teaching and workshops**: Community members run workshops and training sessions worldwide, drawing on talks like [[crossing-the-river-by-feeling-the-stones]]

## Community Character

The Wardley Mapping community has a distinctive character shaped by several factors:
- **Open source ethos**: The CC BY-SA licensing sets the tone — the framework belongs to everyone
- **Practice-oriented**: The community emphasizes doing mapping over theorizing about it
- **Cross-domain**: Participants come from technology, government, military, education, and business backgrounds
- **Informal**: The community is organized around practice and discussion rather than formal certification or credentialing

## Significance

The community is both a vector for the framework's dissemination and a validation of its utility. The fact that practitioners voluntarily adopt, teach, and extend the framework — without commercial incentive from Wardley — suggests it provides genuine value. The community also extends the framework in ways Wardley alone could not, discovering new applications and refining the methodology through diverse practice.
