An educational workbook published by [[north-river-press]] in 2003, accompanied by a CD-ROM simulator. Where [[the-goal]] teaches TOC production concepts through narrative and [[the-race]] presents them as logical diagrams, *Production the TOC Way* provides hands-on practice. The two-part structure — "The Core Problem" and "The Solution" — walks practitioners through translating [[the-goal]]'s insights into operational procedures, with the simulator allowing users to experiment with [[drum-buffer-rope]] scheduling and [[buffer-management]] in a risk-free environment.

[[eliyahu-goldratt]] developed this as a bridge between understanding TOC conceptually and implementing it on the shop floor. The workbook format reflects a gap that practitioners consistently reported: they understood the theory from the novels but struggled with the mechanics of implementation. A companion tutor guide was also published for instructors. The work belongs to the [[domain-expansion-era]] and represents Goldratt's recognition that different learners need different pedagogical entry points — not everyone learns best from [[socratic-novel-pedagogy]].
