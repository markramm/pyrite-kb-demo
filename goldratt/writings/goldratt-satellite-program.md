The Goldratt Satellite Program (1999) was a video lecture series produced and distributed by [[goldratt-institute]] to study groups around the world. Rather than a book or novel, it was a direct transmission mechanism: [[eliyahu-goldratt]] on screen, working through [[theory-of-constraints]] principles, [[thinking-processes]], and implementation challenges with the kind of Socratic energy that participants in his live workshops described as transformative.

The program was structured around 8 distinct modules, each addressing a different domain of management: operations, finance, project management, supply chain, marketing, sales, managing people, and strategy. This structured curriculum gave practitioners a systematic path through TOC's full scope rather than the partial picture available from any single book, and made the program useful both as an introduction for newcomers and as a reference for practitioners who needed depth in a specific area.

The program's significance lies in what it reveals about how TOC actually spread. Books reached individuals; the Satellite Program reached organizations. Study groups — typically assembled by practitioners already familiar with [[the-goal]] or [[critical-chain]] — would gather to watch sessions and then work through the material together. This format created communities of practice in companies and regions that could not easily access Goldratt or [[goldratt-institute]] consultants directly.

The launch of the Satellite Program corresponds to the [[launch-of-satellite-program]] event and belongs to the [[domain-expansion-era]], when Goldratt was actively trying to scale TOC education beyond what any single consulting organization could deliver. In retrospect, it anticipates later distributed learning formats by nearly a decade. The program also covered material — particularly on [[thinking-processes]] and the [[throughput-world-vs-cost-world]] distinction — that was more technically demanding than the novels, serving practitioners who wanted to go deeper than narrative could take them.
