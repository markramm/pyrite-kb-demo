# Blank — Steve Blank Intellectual Biography KB

Knowledge base for an intellectual biography of **Steve Blank** (b. 1953), serial entrepreneur, educator, and creator of the Customer Development methodology — the intellectual foundation of the Lean Startup movement.

Blank's career spans military electronic warfare (USAF 1972-76), 21 years at 8 Silicon Valley startups, and a second career as an educator who transformed how entrepreneurship is taught. His central insight — that a startup is not a small version of a large company — led to Customer Development, a methodology for discovering and validating business models through direct customer contact. The methodology has been adopted by 100+ universities, the NSF (I-Corps), the Department of Defense (Hacking for Defense), and the global startup ecosystem.

## Key Intellectual Themes

- **Search vs. execute** — Startups search for business models; large companies execute them
- **Get out of the building** — Hypotheses must be tested against customer reality, not refined internally
- **Customer Development** — Four-step iterative process: Discovery, Validation, Creation, Building
- **Pivot** — Changing business model hypotheses based on evidence, not failure
- **Business model as hypothesis** — Everything the founder believes is a guess until tested
- **Scientific method for entrepreneurship** — Treating business model validation as experimental science
