The Leanpub Frontmatter Podcast interview with [[gerald-weinberg]], conducted by Len Epp and published December 19, 2017, stands as one of Weinberg's last recorded interviews before his death in [[gerald-weinberg-death-2018]] eight months later. The Frontmatter series focused on authors who published through [[leanpub]], the self-publishing platform Weinberg had adopted after the wind-down of [[dorset-house-publishing]].

The interview covers Weinberg's transition to self-publishing, his writing process as described in [[weinberg-on-writing-2006]] and the [[fieldstone-method]], and his views on what had changed—and what had not—in software development over his career. Weinberg discusses his late-career works including [[change-artistry-2013]] and [[agile-impressions-2013]], and reflects on the community of practice he had built through events like [[aye-conference]] and workshops like [[psl-workshop-founded-1974]].

As one of his final substantial interviews, the Leanpub Frontmatter conversation has unusual documentary value. It captures Weinberg's voice, his continued intellectual vitality, and his perspective on a career spanning from [[ibm-and-project-mercury-1956-1969]] to the self-publishing era—a trajectory that saw software development transform almost beyond recognition while Weinberg's core commitments to human judgment, systems thinking, and [[congruent-behavior]] remained stable.
