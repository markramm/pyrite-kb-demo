The final phase of [[gerald-weinberg]]'s career, characterized by three threads: community building through the [[aye-conference]], a prolific self-publishing program through [[leanpub]], and the consolidation of a legacy that would be recognized by the software engineering and testing communities.

## AYE Conference and Teaching

Weinberg co-founded the [[aye-conference]] (Amplifying Your Effectiveness) with [[esther-derby]] and [[johanna-rothman]], providing a venue for experiential learning in the Weinberg tradition. AYE was not a typical software conference — it was workshop-based, emphasizing the kind of immersive, experiential learning that Weinberg had developed through the Problem Solving Leadership (PSL) workshop and the Change Shop. The conference became a gathering point for the community of practitioners shaped by Weinberg's ideas, and it produced [[amplifying-your-effectiveness-2000]], a collected volume from the community. He also maintained the [[shape-forum]], an online community that generated the two Roundtable dialogue volumes.

## Self-Publishing and Leanpub

Weinberg embraced [[leanpub]] self-publishing as a way to reach readers directly, bypassing the long production cycles of traditional publishing. This produced a stream of works: [[more-secrets-of-consulting-2001]], [[weinberg-on-writing-2006]], [[perfect-software-2008]], [[agile-impressions-2013]], [[do-you-want-to-be-a-better-manager-2016]], [[errors-bugs-boo-boos-blunders-2017]], and the four-volume experiential learning series. He also expanded the original Quality Software Management series into a reorganized multi-volume Quality Software series — including [[how-software-is-built-2010]], [[how-to-observe-software-systems-2010]], [[responding-to-significant-software-events-2011]], and [[change-planned-and-unplanned-2014]] — and wrote several novels including the Aremac science fiction series. He maintained the [[secretsofconsulting-blog|Secrets of Consulting blog]] as a late-career channel for reaching readers directly, and published [[exploring-requirements-two-2011]] with [[donald-gause]].

## Recognition and Legacy

The [[stevens-award-2000|Stevens Award]] (2000) and the [[eurostar-testing-excellence-award-2013|EuroSTAR Testing Excellence Award]] (2013) recognized his contributions to software engineering and testing respectively. The [[luminary-award-2010|Luminary Award]] (2010) added further recognition. Most meaningful to Weinberg was [[gift-of-time-2008]], a festschrift by students and colleagues edited by [[fiona-charles]] — seventeen essays from practitioners whose work Weinberg had shaped.

Weinberg died August 7, 2018, leaving a legacy that runs through the Agile movement (whose first value — "individuals and interactions over processes and tools" — is essentially his thesis), the context-driven testing community (via [[james-bach]], [[michael-bolton]], and [[cem-kaner]]), and organizational development practice (via [[esther-derby]], [[johanna-rothman]], and the broader consulting community he trained). The [[psl-reader-2020]], published posthumously by Derby, Rothman, and [[don-gray]], preserved the intellectual inheritance of his most influential workshop.
