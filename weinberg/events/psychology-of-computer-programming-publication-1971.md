Publication of [[psychology-of-computer-programming-1971]], the first book to treat programming as a human activity. Introduced [[egoless-programming]] and launched the field of programming psychology. At the time, the book received modest attention — the computing profession was focused on hardware, algorithms, and formal methods. Its influence grew over decades as the software industry matured and recognized that team dynamics, communication, and ego management were central to software quality.
