[[gerald-weinberg]] died on August 7, 2018, at the age of 84. His death was announced by Sue Petersen on Facebook, and the news spread rapidly across the software development community, prompting an outpouring of tributes from practitioners, authors, consultants, and educators whose careers had been shaped by his work.

Weinberg had been in declining health for several years; [[don-gray]] had taken over facilitation of the [[psl-workshop-founded-1974]] in 2017 as Weinberg could no longer lead it himself. His death came roughly a decade after the celebration of [[gift-of-time-festschrift-2008]] and two decades after the [[stevens-award-2000]], by which time his place in the history of software engineering was secure. He had continued writing and publishing through [[leanpub]] in his final years, including [[change-artistry-2013]] and [[psl-reader-2020]].

Tributes poured in from across the community. [[james-bach]] wrote a widely circulated piece titled "Jerry Weinberg's Last Worry," captured in [[james-bach-weinberg-tribute-2018]], describing the depth of the mentorship Weinberg had provided. [[infoq-weinberg-obituary-2018]] by Ben Linders provided a formal professional record. The response reflected the unusual reach of Weinberg's influence: he had touched software testing through figures like Bach and [[michael-bolton]], organizational change through [[esther-derby]] and [[johanna-rothman]], and software engineering broadly through decades of books published by [[dorset-house-publishing]].
