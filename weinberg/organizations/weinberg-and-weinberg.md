[[gerald-weinberg]] and [[dani-weinberg]]'s consulting practice, founded in 1969 when Jerry left IBM after thirteen years at [[ibm-federal-systems-division]]. Through Weinberg & Weinberg, they delivered consulting, training, and workshops to software organizations for nearly five decades.

## Scope of Work

The firm's consulting work focused on the human and organizational dimensions of software development — the territory Weinberg had staked out in [[psychology-of-computer-programming-1971]] and expanded throughout his career. Engagements typically addressed organizational culture, management effectiveness, team dynamics, and the interpersonal patterns that determine software quality. The [[quality-software-management-framework]] provided the diagnostic framework; [[congruent-behavior]] and the [[satir-change-model]] provided the intervention tools.

## Workshops

The firm's most significant offerings were its workshops, particularly the Problem Solving Leadership (PSL) workshop ([[psl-workshop-founded-1974]]) and the Change Shop. PSL became the capstone of Weinberg's teaching practice — an intensive, residential, experiential program that produced over 3,000 graduates across four decades. The Change Shop, conceived with [[virginia-satir]] in 1988 and continued after her death by [[jean-mclendon]], brought family systems therapy techniques into organizational settings.

## Influence

Weinberg & Weinberg operated as a small, relationship-driven practice rather than scaling into a large consulting firm. This was consistent with Weinberg's own consulting philosophy — the [[helpful-model-of-consulting]] emphasizes building client capability rather than creating consultant dependency, which does not optimize for revenue growth. The firm's influence was disproportionate to its size: many of its alumni — including [[esther-derby]], [[johanna-rothman]], [[james-bach]], and [[don-gray]] — went on to build their own practices, carrying Weinberg's approach into new organizations and communities.
