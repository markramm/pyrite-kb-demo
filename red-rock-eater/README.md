# Red Rock Eater — Philip Agre Intellectual Biography KB

Knowledge base for an intellectual biography of **Philip E. Agre** (1960–), AI researcher, social theorist, and author of the Red Rock Eater News Service newsletter (1993–2003).

Agre's work spans AI and cognitive science, critical theory of technology, political engagement through networked communication, and the social dimensions of computation. He is known for his critique of planning-based AI, the concept of "critical technical practice," the capture model of surveillance and privacy, and his influential analysis of how institutions shape and are shaped by information technology.

## Entry Types

- **writings/** — Papers, RRE posts, book chapters, talks, interviews
- **concepts/** — Key ideas and frameworks (critical technical practice, capture model, grammars of action, deictic representation, etc.)
- **people/** — Intellectual network: collaborators, mentors, interlocutors, influences
- **organizations/** — Institutions: MIT AI Lab, UCLA, UCSD, conferences, mailing lists
- **events/** — Biographical and intellectual events
- **eras/** — Periods of intellectual development
- **sources/** — Secondary sources about Agre
- **notes/** — Research notes and analysis

## Key Eras

1. **MIT AI Lab period** (~1981–1992) — PhD work, critique of planning, Pengi, deictic representation
2. **UCSD transition** (~1992–1996) — "Toward a Critical Technical Practice," computation and human experience
3. **UCLA and RRE peak** (~1996–2002) — Surveillance and capture, institutional analysis, political engagement, newsletter at full stride
4. **Withdrawal** (~2002–2009) — Gradual retreat from public life, disappearance
