```

PRESS RELEASE
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
May 16, 1994

ANALYTICAL REVIEW OF THE LIBRARY OF THE FUTURE
Available from the Council on Library Resources

The Council on Library Resources announces the
publication of Analytical Review of the Library of the
Future.

In order to provide a synthesis of the current thinking
about the library of the future, Dr. Karen M.
Drabenstott, University of Michigan School of
Information and Library Studies, examined published
literature of the library and publishing communities
from the early 1980's to date.  An extensive
bibliography was compiled and the Analytical Review of
the Library of the Future summarizes the trends,
topics, issues, and concerns identified by the many
individuals writing about the library of the future.
Included in the report is a collection of definitions,
overviews of current technologies and tools, a
discussion of digital library models, brief
descriptions of active projects, and a concluding
thinkpiece on the future that incorporates observations
based on the literature.  The report includes a
bibliography of the cited literature (about half of the
citations collected) as well as a combined author-
subject index.  Research assistance for the project was
provided by Celeste M. Burman.

Published copies of the Analytical Review of the
Library of the Future (200 pages bound and printed)
are available from the Council on Library Resources for
$15.00 prepaid. (International orders, please add $5.00
for additional shipping charges.)

Diskettes (3.5-inch) of the Pro-Cite (TM) bibliography
of the library of the future literature (375 entries)
also may be obtained from the Council on Library
Resources for $5.00 prepaid.

Send prepaid orders to:  Council on Library Resources
                    1400 16th St. N.W., Suite 510
                    Washington, DC 20036-2217

The Analytical Review and its associated Pro-Cite(TM)
bibliography of library-of-the-future literature may be
obtained over the Internet using anonymous FTP.  The
FTP host name is "sils.umich.edu."  When the system
asks you for a user name and a password, type the user
name "anonymous" and type your electronic mail address
(or last name) for the password.  The directory in
which the documents are located is /pub/papers/CLR.

Although the files in the anonymous FTP directory are
made available in several formats, it is strongly
suggested that you obtain the Review in Postscript or
PDF (Adobe Acrobat) file formats to preserve layout,
paging, and type fonts.  The formats of fields in the
anonymous FTP directory are as follows:

File Names          Description and Format:

CLRRVW.pdf     Review in PDF (Adobe Acrobat) format
CLRRVW.ps      Review in Postscript format
CLRRVW.ASC     Review in ASCII format
CLRRVW.sit.hqx Review in Microsoft Word 5.1a (Mac,
                stuffed and bin-hexed)
CLRBIB.ASC     Bibliography in ASCII format (warning:
                long lines)
CLRBIB.ZIP      Bibliography in DOS ProCite format,
                ZIPed

To retrieve the CLRRVW.sit.hqx file, you will need a
copy of the BinHex and UnStuffIt utilities for
Macintosh.  These are free, public-domain utilities
that can be obtained via anonymous FTP from
"archive.umich.edu" in the directory
/mac/utilities/compression.  To use the file, you will
need Microsoft Word 5.1a for the Macintosh.  To unzip
the CLRBIB.ZIP file, you will need a copy of UNZIP,
which can be found at the anonymous FTP site
"archive.umich.edu" in the directories
/msdos/compress/zip (DOS) and /mac/util/compression
(Mac).  To use the files, you will need a copy of Pro-
Cite(TM) for DOS, which is available from Personal
Bibliographic Software, Inc.
```
