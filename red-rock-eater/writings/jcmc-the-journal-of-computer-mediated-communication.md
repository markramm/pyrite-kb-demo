Subject:      JCMC, the Journal of Computer Mediated Communication

We are pleased to announce the establishment of a new scholarly
publication, The Journal of Computer-Mediated Communication (JCMC).

JCMC is a joint project of the Annenberg School for Communication,
University of Southern California, and the Information Systems
Division of the School of Business Administration, Hebrew University
of Jerusalem. The Editors are Margaret  McLaughlin and Sheizaf
Rafaeli.  McLaughlin and Rafaeli are Co-Editors, with Fay Sudweeks,
of the forthcoming Network and NetPlay:The Virtual Group on the
Internet (AAAI/MIT Press).

JCMC will be a scientific, refereed, quarterly journal. It is lead
by an editorial board composed of leading scholars in the fields
of CMC. The journal seeks to publish original essays and research
reports on such topics as interpersonal and group processes in
communication networks, issues of privacy, economics and access
raised by the developing information infrastructure, instructional
communication in distance learning, computer-supported cooperative
work, and organizational and social policy issues occasioned by
the new medium.

JCMC will be distributed as a hypermedia document on the World Wide
Web, and can also be accessed as plain text through gopher and ftp.
Contributors will be able to include illustrative materials such
as photographs, color graphics, sound clips, and short video clips,
and as well to provide hyperlinks to related documents such as
annotated bibliographies, archived data, and journals in cognate
fields. Readers without access to Web browsers such as Mosaic, Lynx
or Cello can retrieve articles and their associated binary files
through ftp or a gopher server.  Other features of the WWW version
of JCMC will be a group annotations server for providing comments
on articles, and keyword searches of current and archived texts.
We hope to hold online miniconferences surrounding special issues
as well.

The full text of the announcement of JCMC, its editorial intent,
a complete list of editorial board members, and submission guidelines
are available online. Our online resource now contains a preliminary
CMC bibliography, and pointers to other CMC study resources. We
hope to have the full program of the upcoming ICA annual conference
within a few days.

To read more about JCMC, via World Wide Web (www, Mosaic, Cello,
Lynx, or any other reader) go http://www.huji.ac.il/www_jcmc/jcmc.html

Or via ftp at:  noga.huji.ac.il, directory jcmc. Download the JCMC
file.

An additional Web server and Ftp site are expected to open at the
University of Southern California shortly.

For further information, send e-mail to mmclaugh@alnitak.usc.edu
or sheizafr@shum.cc.huji.ac.il.

Please pay us a visit!
