     The W. Alton Jones Foundation and the Telecommunications Cooperative 
     Network (tcn) have just finished our public report on the electronic 
     networking project we undertook for advocates working on low and zero 
     emission vehicle (LEV-ZEV) issues during 1995-96.  
     
          "Circuit Riders: Pioneers in Non-Profit Networking"
     
     You may visit the report on the Jones Foundation's web site at the 
     following address: 
     
          http://www.wajones.org/circuit.html
     
     This report discusses how technical assistance "Circuit Riders" can be 
     a very successful way to promote the more effective use of technology 
     in the non-profit sector.
     
     To request a copy in the mail, you can do one of the following:
     
          1) Use the order form on the Web site; or

     The Foundation's director, J.P. Myers, wrote the following 
     introduction to the report: 
     ==========================================================
     New information technologies create remarkable opportunities for 
     foundations and other non-profit organizations.  Even with limited 
     communications dollars, advanced networking tools can increase 
     markedly the effectiveness of our communications.  
     
     Unfortunately, most non-profits remain underachievers in the 
     information technology world.  And when we do invest in technology, we 
     fail to make a corresponding investment in the human resources needed 
     to make the equipment perform.
     
     At the W. Alton Jones Foundation, we have found a productive way to 
     help grantees communicate more effectively: "circuit riders"--talented 
     individuals who provide hands-on, technical consulting and education 
     to non-profit organizations.  
     
     The foundation had begun an ambitious program to support advocates of 
     Low Emission Vehicles and Zero Emission Vehicles (LEV-ZEV).  This was 
     one component of the foundation's commitment to encouraging 
     wide-spread adoption of hydrogen fuel cells as 
     environmentally-friendly sources of energy.  Early on, it became 
     evident that electronic communication would facilitate effective 
     cooperation among the network of organizations working in California, 
     New England and the mid-Atlantic on LEV-ZEV.  Our circuit-rider proved 
     highly effective in bringing these advocates quickly to a position of 
     making effective use of computer communications.
     
     The success of this LEV-ZEV networking project led us to several 
     important conclusions:

* Content drives communication--serious networks go beyond surfing by 
  connecting advocates to mission-critical information and resources;* Our investment enhanced the impact of other grants we made to these 
    environmental advocates; and* It would have cost much more to achieve the same results had each 
      LEV-ZEV organization sought its own individual technical consultant.

For a partner in this project, we chose tcn, a communication 
     technology cooperative. As a member of tcn, the Jones Foundation knew 
     that a tcn circuit rider would bring important knowledge and resources 
     to the participants.  Indeed, tcn successfully changed the profile of 
     the LEV-ZEV organizations' use of new communications technologies.
     
     We welcome your feedback and encourage you to contact both tcn and the 
     W. Alton Jones Foundation. This report is also available on the 
     Internet at WAJF's World Wide Web site: 
     http://www.wajones.org/circuit.html
     
     J.P. Myers, Ph.D.
     Director, W. Alton Jones Foundation
