# be a friend of the red rock eater

```
Some administrative notes.

Many people ask me, "how do you ever get the time to run the Red Rock
Eater?"  These people seem to believe that it takes me hours a day to
run the list, and sometimes they seem to imply that I must be shirking
my real job.  The fact is, RRE takes minutes a day to run.  Why?  Because
Mike Corrigan does most of the technical stuff for me -- and because I
get much of what I send to RRE from RRE subscribers.  Many other people
have told me, "I didn't send you such-and-such because you seem so well
connected that I assumed you already had it".  The fact is, most of my
"connections" are RRE subscribers.

Therefore, I invite all RRE subscribers to send me occasional items that
might be good for the list.  I realize that this is tricky, given that
the final criterion is whether I find the item interesting in my guts.
But if you come across something that you think deserves wide circulation
and is not copyrighted, please consider sending it along.  (All wire
service reports are copyrighted, by the way.  All messages contributed
to Internet discussion groups are automatically copyrighted by their
authors, but it's easy enough to ask them for permission to forward the
message to others.)

I'm particularly hoping to get more technical and industry analyses of
the Internet and related technologies, more material on human rights,
and more announcements and documents relating to technology politics in
countries outside of North America.  But anything that you find once-a-
year interesting is probably worth sending along, particularly if you
think it will help expand other's horizons.

On a different topic, I'm fairly happy with the "tag" that now begins 
most RRE messages.  It has reduced the number of misdirected replies to
items I forward to the list, and it has reduced but not eliminated the
troubles associated with the otherwise excellent Eudora's evil "redirect"
command.  It has not, though, reduced the number of people who ask
me to take them off the list without any suggestion that they've tried
doing it themselves.  It's now my policy simply to delete such messages.

I hope you're enjoying RRE.  I won't tell you what the weather is like
in San Diego today.

Phil
```
