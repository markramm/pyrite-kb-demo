The Massachusetts Institute of Technology invites you to join us on
Saturday, November 21, 1998, for the final day of the Junior Summit
'98, a pioneering effort to give children from all corners of the
globe, and all walks of life, a voice in the future.

Thousands of children ages 10 to 16 from more than 130 countries are
already participating in the summit through an on-line discussion
forum via the Internet, breaking down barriers of distance, economics,
and languages, to create a world community of children.  They are
exploring such pressing issues as child rights, education, child
labor, health care, environmental responsibility, and world peace.
This event marks an important beginning, one that will bring forth the
best and brightest ideas from the world's first Internet generation
and connect young people in a way never before possible.

Later this month, 100 of these participants will come to MIT for a
one-week meeting, which will conclude on the 21st, with the children
presenting their ideas to world leaders best positioned to carry
these concepts forward.  We would be honored if you would be able
to join global executives and world leaders for this important event.

Please check the Junior Summit website http://www.jrsummit.net for
updated information on the event, a listing of the special guests, and
the conference schedule.

The event will take place from 9 am - 12:30 pm on November 21 at MIT's
Kresge Auditorium. 
Please RSVP at http://www.jrsummit.net/rsvp, by email at rsvp@jrsummit.net,
or by fax to 617-252-6995.
