                        Tel*Ed/Multimedia '96

         We are looking for your help to bridge the distances....

This is an open invitation to each of you to join us in creating
Tel*Ed & Multimedia '96. Together we can create an innovative
educational experience, different from what has existed in the
past.  We envision a conference where communication technology
is used to create learning experiences for the participants.  We
envision a conference where participants show, not tell, how
telecommunication can bridge distances,  a conference where
guided learning excursions replace the succession of sages on
the stage.

Tel*Ed/Multimedia '96 will be held simultaneously in two sites,
Tampa Florida, and Monterrey Mexico on Dec. 5-8th. A central
message conveyed at education and telecommunication conferences
is that technology conquers distances and creates global partnerships.
Our goal is to work toward creating an annual conference that
SHOWS how telecommunication conquers distance and creates partnerships.

Tel*Ed/Multimedia '96 (our fifth conference) is our first
effort at a multi-year experiment to recreate conferences in a
technology rich environment.

In the years that follow we hope to add more sites making Tel*Ed/Multimedia
into a conference that is held simultaneously worldwide in both
regional gatherings and on the Internet.  (An example of an online
conference is the Governor's Conference for the Arts VII: The Role
of the Artist in the Digital Age <http://www.well.com/user/govconf/>).

We want all of you to help us find ways to bridge distances so that
participants in Tampa Florida and Monterrey Mexico will share the
experience of being at Tel*Ed/Multimedia '96.

The call for participation <http://isteonline.uoregon.edu> describes
the new sessions for this conference:

        Education Bridges
        World Wide Web Tours
        People linking Across Projects
        Talk of the Net
        Net Results
        Workshops

We hope that you will find these session descriptions helpful in
formulating your plans for creating these learning excursions.

                         Educational Bridges
                        /-------------------\
 /\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/                     \/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\/\

Ideas both possible and ambitious are part of the dialog. However trans-
lating these plans to actions will require nothing short of the creative
energy of the whole education community.  We need the partnership of those
who have technology products to demonstrate, those that have services to
offer, and those who have the expertise to connect technology to future
designs for learning, teachers and learners. If you want to help transform
these ideas into actions for either this year, or future years please
join us!

"Educational Bridges" sessions are open in terms of design.  We want and
 need your ideas and help.  Here are some ideas that we have discussed:

Electronic Travel:  Take the conference participants on an adventure.
    Instead of a description of how electronic travel can make
    students feel they have left the classroom, transport the conference
    participants to Antarctica, or a rain forest, or Mars. Guide
    conference participants in the same way that you hope they will
    guide students.

Shared Keynotes: Who is on-stage and who is on camera?  Can we do as
    well as the McNeal Lehrer Newshour in bringing people who are
    physically in two different locations into the same discussion
    space? Please share your ideas for how we might use the keynote
    whole group sessions in two sites to accomplish our goals.

Virtual Reality: Can we see the other site at the end of an Internet
    tunnel. Can we have a large group meeting mediated by the Internet?
    What would such a meeting look like?  How would you organize it?
    What is shared networking space and what are the ways we demonstrate it?

             Bridging the U.S. and Mexico Conference Sites
            /----------------------------------------------\
 /\/\/\/\/\/                                                \/\/\/\/\/\/\/

We speak different languages and have many different cultural and political
realities, but we share a interest in putting our more creative tools to
work in our schools.  And this conference is a partnership that evolves
from this shared interest.  We want the participants at both sites to be a
part of a growing global community focused in teaching and learning in
the 21st century. What can we do to foster the sense of partnership?
Here again we turn to you for help.  These are some rough ideas of what
might be possible but many of you will have your own visions and we hope
you will work to share them with us.

Shared Space:  At the Monterrey site, we want to have a "Tampa Room"
    and at the Tampa site, a "Monterrey Room."  Ideally these rooms
    will be connected by a range of technology so that being in the
    room at either site would feel like being in the same space.

Through the Looking Glass: One wall could be a mirror reflection
    with live video of the people across the shared space.  You would
    see and perhaps hear the people in the linked rooms.

Let's Chat: Video conferencing stations could be reserved for individual
    and small group meetings with people at the other conference.
    You might schedule a meeting at multimedia station 5 at 10:30
    for a 15 minute chat with a colleague at Tel*Ed.

Milling Around: Another large wall at each site could project video
    pictures (live or recorded and transmitted) of people as they wonder
    through the public spaces of the distant site.  This would be a
    visual reminder of who is "here" and "there."

Message Me!: An email conferencing system could link two group so
    that you could easily reach someone who you saw wandering around
    at either site.

Without your help, none of these ideas will make it off this page. Remember:

                        Conferences are the collective
                        expression of a community.

Read the "Call for Participation" carefully and then please take an
active role in creating Tel*Ed/Multimedia '96.  We are eager to hear
from you!
