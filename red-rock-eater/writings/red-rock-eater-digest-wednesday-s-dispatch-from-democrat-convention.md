Notes on Al Gore's Reinvention Convention

Al Gore's efforts to reinvent his record continue.  Today Democrats
will attempt to recast Al Gore as a principled fighter.  But Al Gore
has repeatedly failed to fight for working families.

Despite nearly a decade of uninterrupted economic growth, Clinton-Gore
have squandered bipartisan opportunities on health care, tax fairness,
education, and other key issues.  As a result, millions of Americans
have been left behind.

The bitter partisan tone in Washington is a roadblock to reforms that
would mean a healthy, more secure future for millions of American
children.  Millions of Americans face the fear of losing their health
insurance and joining the ranks of the more than 44 million Americans
who are uninsured today.

The number of Americans without health coverage has risen by eight
million under Clinton-Gore.  Clinton and Gore have had years of
opportunities to give families better access to affordable health
coverage, and they've squandered them.

Clinton and Gore have repeatedly killed bipartisan reforms that would
make the tax code more reasonable and fair for middle-income families.
For cynical political reasons, Al Gore and this administration have
allowed grossly-unfair tax laws like the marriage penalty and the
death tax to stand.

The marriage penalty slaps an estimated 25 million American families
with an average of $1,400 a year in higher taxes simply for making
the decision to get married.  Legislation to eliminate the marriage
penalty has repeatedly passed with bipartisan support, only to be
terminated by the Clinton-Gore veto pen.

Gore opposes bipartisan education reforms that would help to close the
widening achievement gap in education between disadvantaged students
and their peers.  Gore's own running mate supports some of these
reforms - but he's being pressured to change his positions to allow
Gore to continue his political attacks.

Americans want to change the tone in Washington so we can tackle the
big issues facing our nation.  No child should be left behind.
