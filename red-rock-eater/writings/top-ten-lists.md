# top ten lists

```
Date: Tue, 30 Aug 1994 00:10:59 -0400
From: Alan Bawden <Alan@lcs.mit.edu>
Subject: 25 thousand subscribers!

Now here's a weird case of the Internet interacting with more extablished
media.  What this guy was doing was electronicly distributing Dave
Letterman's Top Ten list every night.  He wasn't getting them in advance or
anything -- he was just typing them in after the fact.  Looks like he woke
up one morning, realized he had 25,000 subscribers (!), and started to
worry about all those big bad lawyers at CBS.  (Or maybe CBS woke up one
morning...)

If CBS is smart, they'll realize what good advertising this is for them,
and let him continue.

Date:         Mon, 29 Aug 1994 22:48:41 -0600
Reply-To: Late Show Top Ten List mailing list <TOP-TEN@tamvm1.tamu.edu>
From: top-ten <top10@genesis.tamu.edu>
Subject:      The TOP-TEN Listserv
To: Multiple recipients of list TOP-TEN <TOP-TEN@tamvm1.tamu.edu>

Greetings,

     I'm sad to announce that the TOP-TEN listserv will be going off the air
temporarily.  Since the list has grown to over 25 thousand subscribers (making
it one of, if not the, largest listserv lists in existence) we are reviewing
our legal status with CBS and have decided to suspend transmissions of the list
until we receive confirmation from them that we may continue.  Once we are
given the green light once again we plan to send out all of the lists that you
may miss during our absence.  Once again, I regret that this has become
necessary.  The listserv will still be functioning during the interim
accepting subscribe and unsubscribe requests.  With a little luck, we'll have
the lists going out again soon!

     Also, listserv began sending out renewal messages to subscribers who
have been on the list for 3 months or more last night but we have suspended
the renewal process until we resume sending out the lists each day.  You may
safely ignore the renewal notice, contrary to what it says, you will not be
removed from the list if you don't forward it back.  Renewals will resume when
we begin distributing the list again.

                                           Stanley
```
