# Scientology, RRE

```
The conflict between the Church of Scientology and its opponents on
the Internet has been accelerating lately.  See the following URL's:

  http://www.gate.net/~shipbrk/Co$/flag-rpt.html
  http://www.cybercom.net/~rnewman/scientology/home.html

I encourage everyone to inform themselves and others about the Church
and the controversies surrounding it.

On a happier note, RRE now welcomes its first subscribers from Botswana,
Lithuania, Malta, Mauritius, Mozambique, Slovakia, Ukraine, and Uruguay.
About 3800 people now subscribe directly, and about a dozen sites run
local RRE distribution lists.

Many people, I gather, are still unaware of the meaning of the phrase
"red rock eater".  It's explained in the FAQ, but here it is anyway.
It comes from Bennett Cerf's Riddle Book:

  Q: What's big and red and eats rocks?
  A: A big red rock eater.

I thought this was hysterical when I was four years old, and I still do.  
Here is a slightly more mature sequel from a subscriber's five-year-old
in New Zealand:

  Q: What's big and red and eats sand?
     [Typical stupid grown-up answer: "A big red sand eater?".  Duh...]
  A: A big red rock eater on a diet.

The phrase "red rock eater", in short, means nothing at all.  I simply
wanted to choose as un-computer-like a name as I possibly could.

Phil
```
