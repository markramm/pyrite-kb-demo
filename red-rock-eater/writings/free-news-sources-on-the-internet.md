               USING THIS GUIDE

This guide is designed to encourage local sites to create their
own news services. Hopefully such sites will add local news and
the whole package will be made available on the Internet.
All the primary sources listed here provide significant news on a
daily basis - without charge. Some have archives which are
invaluable to researchers. In addition, information is included to
let you respond to traditional news sources in the spirit of
network interactivity.
This includes info on Internet accessible :
          Newspapers - U.S. and Foreign
          TV & RADIO stations
          Press Services and Media Networks
          Publications interested in announcements of new internet
               based news sources.
Finally, I have also included directions for locating the providers
of all newspapers that are available in electronic form from a fee
based DAILY NEWS source. Internet access to e-text versions of
nearly 200 US & foreign daily newspapers is available from
commercial sources.

If you decide to build your own news service, remember that the
internet is a perfect resource from which to build specialty
sections. You can easily obtain sports events lists, recipes,
nutrition tips, book and film reviews, and most of the other types
of items used to broaden the appeal of printed newspapers. You can
also deliver hypertext and multimedia versions which really explore
the power of this medium. See the entry for aspiring journalists
below!

     The main LISTINGS include the following sections:
     --------------------------------------------------

News Source:   Organization(s) providing the news.
Site:          Internet address for the site. If no access method
               is mentioned - it is a gopher site. For access use
               your local system's gopher client. Ask your system
               administrator for instructions.
Path:          directions for descending the local information tree
               until the NEWS area is reached. Each / is followed
               by the name of the next choice to select as you
               follow the path to the information.
Producer:      Name of the organization responsible for preparing
               this particular version of the material from the
               primary source listed above.
Indexed:       indicates that the day's news is available as a
               one line description of each original article.
               These can be useful as very brief guides to the news
               and as leads to stories worth finding from other
               more detailed sources.
Abstracts:     provides a brief summary of original news items.
Full Text:     The compete original news items are available.
Schedules:     Future programming schedules are provided by some
               sites.
Archive:       Historic files of the daily material are kept. The
               starting date or the normal storage period is shown.
Searchable:    The site allows for searching the entire contents
               of each item. Some sites have searches that
               only retrieve by item date. These are not indicated
               as searchable.

               this database should be used too! It allows
               automatic locating of any other items with the same
               key words.

CYBERNEWS
CyberNews, originating at Cornell University, is the bureau
covering headline news, current events, and other standard news
items. It has a CyberNews WWW Page, which is maintained by
Tobermory Everett (teverett@osiris.ac.hmc.edu).
For regular feeds of material - subscribe to the
CyberNews-Publish-L list. To subscribe to CyberNews-Publish-L, send
an email
to: listserv@cornell.edu containing the message:
SUB CYBERNEWS-PUBLISH-L your name
where "your name" is the name of the individual or organization
posting/receiving the stories.The Cybernews-Publish-L list is open
to individual reporting students as well as schools and
publications.
The CyberNews FTP archive, storing items from the PUBLISH-L list,
GSN FAQs and other related documents is also available at Cornell,
at the IP address: 132.236.225.25

UF NEWSWIRE
The UF Newswire is a mailing list that covers feature stories and
magazine-style articles. Its focus is on longer, more in-depth
studies of a particular topic. It also encourages readers to take
full advantage of the capabilities of the World Wide Web when
making their submissions, i.e including links to pictures,
sounds, or other documents within the text of the submitted
article.
The UF Newswire is maintained at the University of Florida by Gary
Ritzenthaler (garyz@elm.circa.ufl.edu) and the staff of the UF
Electronic News Lab (electnws@jou.ufl.edu). To subscribe to the UF
Newswire, send  an email to
electnws@jou.ufl.edu
with the subject line:
subscribe UF Newswire
and the name of the publication/posting receiving stories.

information about ONLINE versions of the TRADITIONAL PRINT PRESS

88888888888888888888888888888888888888888888888888888888888888888

Source:   Library of Congress
Site:     marvel.loc.gov
Path:     /Global Electronic library / Reference / News & Weather
           / Newspapers (library of Congress) / Bibliographies...
           1 - / FULLTEXT US
           2 - / FULLTEXT INTERNATIONAL
Producer:      Compiled by Betsy Reifsnyder, Congressional
          Reference Division, Congressional Research Service
Content:       The two guides provide lists of all full text
sources for traditional print journals. These can be used to
complete the research done online using sources that only provide
abstracts; or to provide a more local perspective on a story.

There are currently 109 US newspapers available fulltext online
from DataTimes, DIALOG, and NEXIS (Mead Data Central).  Newspapers
are included if they are general-interest (i.e., not focused
specifically on a single topic, e.g., business, legal affairs,
etc.) and if most of the articles from the printed paper are
online.  Fulltext online does not, however, mean cover-to-cover
inclusion.
Wire stories and op-eds/syndicated columns are two major types of
items that may not be included in online files.  Inclusion vary
by paper.  Other exclusions generally comprise financial tables
(e.g., stock listings, exchange rates, etc.), sports statistics,
weather forecasts, graphics and photos, advertisements, TV-radio-
movie listings, and fillers and other minor items.
The papers are listed in order by state.  File names are given for
each newspaper and state group file names are also given.

A growing number of non-US newspapers are available fulltext
online. The online versions are in English unless otherwise noted.
