February 10, 1995
Dear Colleague:

On behalf of the Bay Area Digital GeoResource (BADGER) program
team and Smart Valley, Inc., it is our pleasure to invite you
and your colleagues to a pair of meetings to exchange ideas, and,
in particular, to evoke suggestions that will help ensure the
success of BADGER.

As you know, BADGER is an internet-based visualization system
for geographic data of the San Francisco Bay Area developed
from satellite and aerial imagery, maps and databases. The
system will be available to municipalities, utilities and other
commercial, environmental and non-profit interests.  Three
high-payoff applications have been selected as candidates to
demonstrate the applied value of imagery and related data sets.
These are non-point source storm water discharge, vegetation
management, and fire hazard prediction and risk assessment.

We have scheduled the two meetings for Monday, February 27th,
at the Mountain View City Council chambers, 500 Castro Street.

The first meeting, "Geographic Information Applications," will
run from 9:00 A.M. until noon and is intended for those who
require or anticipate a need for incorporating geographically
based data into their business operations.

The second meeting, "Mapping, Computing, and Connectivity
Technology," will run from 1:30 P.M. until 4:30 P.M.  Intended
for vendors, technologists, and service suppliers, this
meeting will focus on the BADGER service, including the planned
base-map and tools for access and visualization.

Further information is available below or from the Bay Area
Shared Information Consortium (BASIC) at (415) 528-4084.

Each meeting will begin with an overview of the BADGER program
and detail on the topic of the specific meeting.  The
majority of the meeting will be devoted to listening to your
suggestions and responding to your questions.  Participants
will also be encouraged to complete a survey.  Ideas gathered
through the meetings and survey will be used by the BADGER
team to build a pilot demonstration later in 1995, develop
a more extensive demonstration in 1996, and commence
Bay Area-wide service in 1997.  We also want to enlist
organizations and individuals who wish to work with the program team.

If you would like to submit comments or join the team but
are unable to attend, please fill out the e-mail form below.

Thank you very much for your support of BADGER.  We look
forward to your participation in this key BADGER activity.

Sincerely,
