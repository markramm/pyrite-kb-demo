The URL is httl://www.cin.gov.au. What it is is the home page of the
Community Information Network, a research project of the Australian
federal Department of Social Security set up 18 months ago or so to
explore a number of techno/social policy (to coin a phrase) research
questions - perhaps most specifically the issue of whether access to
on-line services such as the Internet can actually serve to improve
the living standards of low income people.

So the CIN was established to test this proposition, and along the 
way we have become the largest public access Internet network in the 
world, with 480+ public Internet access sites in several States 
across Australia and with 5,500+ registered CIN users. Sitting behind 
the CIN is a comprehensive evaluation framework that will allow us to 
statistically explore whether or not the experiment contributed to 
improving the living statndards of those people who have participated 
in the CIN.

Aside from building an access network in a variety of public
locations (libraries, community centres, migrant resource centres
etc) to provide full Internet access to low income people who may not
be able to afford their own PC/modem, and a dedicated WAN to service
the access network, we have also recently begun to explore the role
effective, tailored, interactive content may play in enhancing the
experiment. So we have developed a re-vamped Web site that we hope 
will engage our users more and add value to their on-line experience 
(in hopefully measurable ways).

It is this site I am inviting you to visit. Please drop by and let us 
know what you (or your readers) think. I would welcome your views. 

Please also take the opportunity to browse some of our content -
particularly the background papers on the CIN (in the CIN Room of
our site) and its evaluation framework. If you haven't heard of us
before, you might find our work interesting.

Hope to see you (in our log files) soon.
