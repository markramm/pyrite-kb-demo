Goood Morning, Full Moon Subscribers!

    Due to travel plans, I'm sending this month's Full Moon
edition a day early.  Enjoy.  The next Full Moon edition will
be on 5/3/96.  Here's the text of last Thursday's Communique:

    Today completes the fifth full year of service from
Computists International.  (TCC 1.01 was published on 4/1/91.)
Of the 35 who signed up for that issue -- other than myself
-- 22 are still members.  I'd like to thank Jerry Brookshire,
Dan Corkill, James Critz, Tim Finin, Leonard Foner,
Enrico Franconi, Bob Futrelle, Ron Gershon, Nahum Goldmann,
Mary Dee Harris, Elizabeth Hinkelman, John Hopkins, John
Josephson, Tod Levitt, Dave Lewis, Calton Pu, Ann Reid,
Mark Rosenstein, Darrell Skinner, Ken Turner, Matthew Witten,
and Bob Woodham for their belief in me and their continuing
support.

    It's Spring Break, so I'm taking next week off.  Look for
TCC, CCJ, and CAJ on 4/9/96.  Have a good vacation!  If you need
anything, just email (or try the search engines listed below).

 1> Research software (in our CRS digest this week):

Free math-related Windows software from Phillips Exeter Academy.

Nutmeg: commercial Smalltalk-style class libraries for Java.

DOS/Windows software demos for NLP, reference, language learning,
and English-Russian machine translation, from Good Language
Software.

PILS: free Windows programming environment to supplant Lisp.

Abuse: source code for a Lisp game.

FreeWalk: VR "meeting room" or "party" environment where
participants can see and hear what other participants
are viewing and saying.

SciPlot: cartesian and polar plotting widget.

Expert Systems Design and Development: book by John Durkin.

Adaption and Learning in Multi-Agent Systems: book
edited by Weiss and Sen.

Intelligent Agents II: book edited by Wooldridge, Mueller,
and Tambe.

Bibliography of automatic text summarization, from Marcos Casa.

 2> Internet job services:

    NSF has a new brochure on Information for Graduate Research
Fellows (NSF 96-42).  FTP nsf9642.txt from stis.nsf.gov.
[grants, 3/25/96.]

    stu-serv-jshop is a UK list about helping students gain
part-time employment.  Send a "join stu-serv-jshop your name"
message to <mailbase@mailbase.ac.uk>.  [<stu-serv-jshop-request
@mailbase.ac.uk>, new-list, 3/14/96.]

    1st Steps in the Hunt is a daily newsletter for job hunters.
See <http://www.interbiznet.com/hunt/>, which also lists
job-hunting resources on the Web.  [<jrs@interbiznet.com>,
net-hap, 3/4/96.]

    4WORK lists US job opportunities, internships, and volunteer
positions throughout the US.  You can ask for email notification
when a suitable job is posted.  <http://www.4work.com>.
[<recruit@4work.com>, net-hap, 4/4/96.]

    On Usenet, the national/international jobs listings
are misc.jobs.offered and misc.jobs.contract.  You might also
try posting to misc.jobs.resumes.

    Resume Robot is a Winsock application that finds
and parses resumes on the Web.  Its database can be used by
job seekers and recruiters.  <http://www.onramp.net/proquest
/resume/robot/robot.html>.  [James Stakelum, AgentNews, 3/12/96.]

    The Job Seeker's Resource Center is
<http://www.interbiznet.com/hunt/>.  [Network News, 2/10/96.]

    Another info site for job seekers is
<http://www.stetson.edu/~hansen/careers.html>.
[<hansen@mbox.stetson.edu>, net-hap, 3/4/96.]

    The Job Search Links page at
<http://members.aol.com/MGoodson2/jobsrch.htm> offers
job listings, recruiters, company profiles, a resume
distribution services, and a salary recalculator.
[Mike Goodson <mgoodson@combase.com>, net-hap, 1/31/96.]

    JobExpress job postings are listed on
<http://ourworld.compuserve.com/homepages/antony_arendt
/jobpost.htm>.  [<72341.456@compuserve.com>, 3/7/96.]

    The Job-Link mailing list is for job announcements
of any kind.  Send a "subscribe" subject line to
<job-link-admin@listserv.job-link.com>.  Resumes may be
posted at <http://www.job-link.com/>, and job ads should be
sent to <job-link@listserv.job-link.com>.  [Charles Boesel
<webguy@job-link.com>, NEW-LIST, 3/15/96.]

    Shawn's Internet Resume Center (SIRC) offers members a home
page and other job services.  <http://www.inpursuit.com/sirc/>.
[WEBster, 3/19/96.]

    IS Opportunities Magazine lists career opportunities
around the world.  <http://ourworld.compuserve.com/homepages
/is_opportunities>.  [<101350.3541@compuserve.com>, newjour,
2/3/96.]

    ELSNET Job Placement Service covers NL and speech research,
primarily for the European communities.  Send job ads
or short CVs (with dates of availability), preferably in English,
to <elsnet-list@let.ruu.nl>.  You can browse job ads at
<http://www.cogsci.ed.ac.uk/elsnet/summary.html>, CVs at
<http://www.cogsci.ed.ac.uk/elsnet/cv-summary.html>,
or search by subject, location, etc., at
<http://wwwots.let.ruu.nl/ELSNET/jobs_and_cvs.html>.  [Dawn
Griesbach <dawn@cogsci.ed.ac.uk>, ELSNET, 3/19/96.  Joe Raben.]

    The National Assoc. of Programmers (NAP) serves programmers,
developers, consultants, and students.  See <http://naponnet.org/>
for benefits.  [<info@naponnet.org>, net-hap, 1/28/96.]

    The National Assoc. of Computer Consultant Businesses (NACCB)
has a job board and resume bank  for computer consultants.
<http://diads.com/rc/owa/jbb.search> or <http://computerwork.com>.
[<naccb@resourcecenter.com>, net-hap, 3/23/96.]

    The National Home Workers Assoc. (NHWA) lists
more than 76K work-from- home job openings in North America,
including many kinds of computer work.
<http://www.homeworkers.com/homeworkers/>.
[<nhwa@homeworkers.com>, net-hap, 2/13/96.]

    North Carolina has a career, jobs, and resume service at
<http://www.webcom.com/nccareer/>.  [<ioshrink@ix.netcom.com>,
net-hap, 2/2/96.]

    Georgia's High Tech Job Bank (and resume database) is
<http://job.sid.net>.  [<webmaster@sid.net>, net-hap, 3/21/96.]

    Texas Job Reports offers employment listings
and career opportunities at <http://www.jobreports.com>.
[<jlgarcia@jobreports.com>, net-hap, 2/3/96.]

    Riccione On-Line CompuSearch lists jobs for
software developers and hardware engineers, plus info on
Texas computer user groups.  <http://www.riccione.com>.
[<riccione@onramp.net>, net-hap, 3/21/96.]

    TOPjobs USA currently has 13K "professional" job listings
in CA, 6K in TX; 2.2K in CO; 2K in AZ; 1.8K in Utah; 700 in
New Mexico; and almost 600 in NV.  Other states are also listed,
for 32K total.  <http://www.fiber.net/topjobs>.  [D. Williams
<williams@fiber.net>, net-hap, 3/22/96.]

  (Use WWW search engines to check for other states, countries,
or specialties.)

 3> Spiders and search engines:

    Prodigy now offers searching of 17M messages from
the past 18 months, by subject, sender, receiver, or keywords.
[St. Petersburg Times, 3/11/96, p. 9.  EDUPAGE.]
(Privacy advocates are concerned about the ease with which
a contributor's participation can be reviewed.  Your postings
may someday be your biography, or evidence in a libel case.)

    ("Printer's ink has been running a race against gunpowder
these many, many years.  Ink is handicapped, in a way, because
you can blow up a man with gunpowder in half a second, while
it may take twenty years to blow him up with a book.  But the
gunpowder destroys itself along with its victim, while a book
can keep on exploding for centuries."  -- Chistopher Morley,
"The Haunted Bookshop."  [Keith Bostic <bostic@bsdi.com>, QOTD,
12/15/95.])

    Andersen Consulting's webpage describes several projects
that monitor newsgroups and other online sources, finding articles
of interest, contact names, and best prices for offered services.
<http://www.ac.com/cstar/hsil/agents/>.  [AgentNews, 3/12/96.]

    Dynamic Mapping is a technique for displaying search hits
from an online database.  <http://www.discribe.ca/mapcover.htm>.
[<lornap@discribe.ca>, net-hap, 3/4/96.]

    World Wide Web Launcher offers links to every major
search engine.  <http://www.flex.net/~greg/launch.htm>.
[<greg@flex.net>, net-hap, 3/12/96.]  Signpost Information
Service also links to many of the WWW search engines.
<http://www.cris.com/~Carroll/>.  [<carroll@cris.com>,
net-hap, 3/4/96.]  StoryNet Page has links to children's sites,
teen sites, software, images, etc., plus many of the new
search engines.  <http://www.users.fast.net/~obe/links.html>.
[Obe <obe@fast.net>, net-hap, 3/22/96.]

    Northwestern College SearchIt includes search engines
for the Internet, gopher, WWW, Usenet newsgroups, daily news,
and general reference services.
<http://www.netins.net/showcase/nwc-iowa/>.  [Floyd Johnson
<floydj@worf.netins.net>, c.i.www.announce, 1/4/96.  net-hap.]

    The Virtual Search Engines page includes the most popular
search engines and tools for searching the Web, newsgroups,
Listserv, FTP, Gopher, email addresses, ZIP codes, etc.
<http://ourworld.compuserve.com/homepages/frankvad/search.htm>.
[<frankvad@dreamscape.com>, net-hap, 2/1/96.]

    Yahoo, <http://www.yahoo.com>, is probably the best
human-organized directory indexing the entire Internet.
The Whole Internet Catalog, <http://nearnet.gnn.com/gnn/wic>,
and Infomine <http://lib-www.ucr.edu/Main.html>, are also
easy-to-use, but less comprehensive.  Two of the best
search engines are InfoSeek, <http://guide.infoseek.com>,
and Alta Vista, <http://www.altavista.digital.com>.
[Fortune, 4/1/96, p. 147.  Flash Information.]

    MOO, Inc. is "the spot to visit if you're interested in
Internet search engines, are an investor looking for the hottest
technology, etc."  <http://access.digex.net/~holycow/index.html>.
[<holycow@access.digex.net>, net-hap, 1/1/96.]

    The WWW Megalist index/search engine is at
<http://www.worcester.com/cgi-bin/megalist/megalist>.
[<root@misty.worcester.com>, net-hap, 1/26/96.]

    REX is another engine for searching a database of website
descriptions.  <http://www.skyline.net/>.  [Thomas Kuegler Jr.
<kuegler@skyline.net>, c.i.www.announce, 2/10/96.  net-hap.]

    BullsEye! is an off-line search tool, to save on
communications costs.  <http://www.siriusquest.com
/bulls/agever.htm>.  [<aen-a1@mv.mv.com>, net-hap, 2/6/96.]
(I presume that it comes with a CD ROM.)

    Eureka! is a simple but powerful Internet search engine.
<http://www.best.com/~mentorms/eureka.htm>.
[<mentorms@best.com>, net-hap, 1/30/96.]

    UCB's fast Inktomi search engine offers relevance retrieval
with MUST (+) and NOT (-) search terms.  Over 2.8M documents
are searched.  <http://inktomi.berkeley.edu/>.  [Scout Report,
2/2/96.]

    InfoScan is a network-based filtering tools for your own
text and email databases.  $35, but with a free demo version
at <http://www.machinasapiens.qc.ca/infoscanang.html>.
[Liz Tompkins <liz@kersur.net>, inet-news, 2/7/96.  net-hap.]

    topicSEARCH from Verity (Mountain View, CA) is a new
text/HTML retrieval application for webmasters using
the Microsoft Internet Information Server.  "Seamlessly
integrated with Microsoft's Internet Server Application
Programming Interface (ISAPI)" to be four times faster
than CGI, with built-in security and administration features.
Distributed free with the Microsoft Internet Business
Development Kit or from <http://www.software.net>
or <http://www.verity.com>.  [WEBster, 2/20/96.]

    CyberDewey accesses Internet resources by the Dewey Decimal
Classification system.  BUBL (Bulletin Board for Library Systems)
does the same using the Universal Decimal Classification system.
See the Scout Toolkit's Search Tools, Subject Catalog section,
on <http://rs.internic.net/scout/toolkit/3b2.html>.  [Scout
Report, 3/1/96.]

    Home Team Advanced Search Site can search multiple
databases on WWW.  <http://www.hometeam.com/>.  [David Gaddis
<creator@hometeam.com>, c.i.www.announce, 1/26/96.  net-hap.]

    Mother Load is a searchable directory of other people's
directories, lists, and web guides, plus a search engine that
can invoke at least nine other search engines.  Stored sections
include Business, Computers, Government, Entertainment,
Medical & Health, Sports & Rec, Travel, World Wide Web resources,
and about 15 very good Mac sites.  Other resource lists are
solicited.  [Matt Hulbert <mhulbert@cosmix.com>, MacWay, 2/28/96.]

    Magellan is a searchable database of reviewed internet sites
(rated * to ****).  You can block sites that had "adult" content
at the time of review.  <http://www.mckinley.com/>.  [Elizabeth
Wellburn <ewellbur@cln.etc.bc.ca>, Network Nuggets, 3/5/96.
net-hap.]

    Infoseek Guide, a free search/browse service, returns
both search hits and a list of topics related to your search
terms.  In-depth news coverage has also been added.
<http://guide.infoseek.com/>.  [Julian Stewart
<julian@infoseek.com>, 3/7/96.  net-hap.]

    Savvy Search can query nineteen search engines, including
Alta Vista, Yahoo, FTPsearch95, the Virtual Software Library,
Excite, Lycos, DejaNews, OKRA (email database), and the Internet
Movie Database.  Boolean and phrase searches are supported.
<http://savvy.cs.colostate.edu:2000/>.  [Scout Report, 2/23/96.]

    Metacrawler is a multithreaded Web searcher that queries
Open Text, Lycos, WebCrawler, InfoSeek, Excite, Inktomi,
Alta Vista, Yahoo, and Galaxy.  It then verifies that each
URL is valid.  <http://metacrawler.cs.washington.edu:8080/>.
[Christine Chiu <pitsco@pitsco.com>, LM_NET, 3/4/96.  net-hap.]

    SEARCH IT!!! offers a very simple interface to at least
ten search engines.  <http://users.why.net/flam/search.html>.
[<flam@why.net>, c.i.www.browsers.misc, 3/14/96.  net-hap.]

    Prime Search is another search interface invoking
Yahoo!, Lycos,  Webcrawler, etc.
<http://www.delta.com/prime.com/pssearch.htm>.
[<70614.635@compuserve.com>, net-hap, 3/23/96.]

    SEARCH.COM from c|net helps you use over 250 search engines,
including Yahoo, Excite, Infoseek, Alta Vista, and many
single-topic databases about cars, movies, recipes, stocks, etc.
You can even set up a personal page with just your favorite
search engines.  <http://www.search.com>.  [Maida Stupski
<maida@cnet.com>, net-hap, 3/18/96.]

    "RES-Links: The All-in-One Resource Page" is a categorized
list of search engines and tools, catalogs, indexes,
databases, locators, resources, guides, and reference materials.
A description is given for each tool.  "If you don't find what
you're looking for, I will do the best I can to help you."
<http://www.cam.org/~intsci/>.  [Sylvain Provencher
<intsci@CAM.ORG>, c.i.www.announce, 3/7/96.  net-hap.]
