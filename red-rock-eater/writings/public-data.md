INTERNATIONAL DATA SOURCES
Laura Guy, Senior Librarian         Gregory Haley,  Head
Data and Program Library Service    Electronic Data Service
University of Wisconsin-Madison     Columbia University

This two-part workshop on international data sources can be
taken as a full-day sequence or independently as half-day sessions.
Each session will consist of a presentation and demonstration of
the material, followed by hands-on practice.  Laura Guy and
Gregory Haley have worked with international data sources for
over 20 years and are active members of the International
Association for Social Science Information Services and
Technology (IASSIST).

Part A: Strategies for Using International        9:00 AM to 12:00 PM
        Data Available from IGOs
Part A will emphasize international data from Inter-Governmental
Organizations (IGOs), such as the United Nations, International
Monetary Fund, World Bank, and the Organization for Economic
Cooperation and Development. Data products will be reviewed
and discussed.  Particular emphasis will be placed on advantages
and disadvantages of each, comparisons of software, availability
of other tools to enhance usage, and sources of assistance.
Discussion will include a review of why various data are
collected by different IGOs and differences in distribution methods.
Handouts offering information on, and links to, the major IGOs and
help resources will  be provided.

Part B: Strategies for Navigating the Internet    1:30 PM to 4:30 PM
        to Locate and Access Data from Other Countries
Part B will emphasize international data from foreign sources
such as national archives and statistical agencies, including
those of developing countries and non-western nations. Particular
attention will be paid to developing search strategies for identifying
data sources, access problems, comparability problems, differences in
collection strategies, and accuracy concerns. Data types include public
microdata as well as other statistical data.  Discussion will include
information on emerging on-line resources via WWW, gopher, etc. Lists
of contacts from whom to obtain further assistance will be provided.

US ECONOMIC DATA
Tom Bell, Survey Statistician
Economic Planning and Coordination Division
Bureau of the Census

This session will provide in-depth information on the economic
data resources of the Census Bureau.  Tom Bell is a survey
statistician with the Bureau of the Census in the Economic
Planning and Coordination Division.  He has been with the
Census Bureau since 1983 in various capacities related to the
economic censuses and surveys and is currently a statistician
for the County Business Patterns data series.

Economic Data at the Census Bureau          1:30 PM to 4:30 PM

This workshop will provide a comprehensive overview of the
data products available from the economic censuses and surveys.
The overview will describe data product content and format.
Participants will be provided with materials for use after the
workshop that will enable them to identify and acquire appropriate
economic data.  In addition, the workshop will describe the Census
Bureau's register of businesses, which is the foundation for the
economic censuses and surveys.  Discussion will help attendees
interpret economic data, explore the potential uses of the business
register for special tabulations, and understand the ramifications of
combining Census Bureau economic data with other resources.

Each workshop is priced at $60 for APDU members, $75 for non-members.
Parts A and B of the International Data Sources workshop are $60 each
($75 each for non-members). Lunch is not provided between Sunday
workshops. Use the registration form at the end of this message to assure
your place at the  Sunday workshops.

WHO SHOULD ATTEND APDU95?

As someone who works with public data, you know that change has
become the operative word. Policies are changing at the federal,
state and local levels. The tools and options for dissemination of
data are changing on a daily basis. Our roles as data collectors,
producers, disseminators and users are changing to meet new demands,
new environments, and new constraints. Through workshops, plenary and
breakout sessions, showcases, and vendor displays, APDU95 will provide
you with the information and tools you need to navigate these changes.- Explore the shifting roles of data access intermediaries and
  end-users and what part we can play in the policy and technical
  developments that shape those roles. * Talk with the statistical
  policy makers and public data producers about the present and the
  future.  * Find out about the present state of statistical policy and
  where it's headed. * Discuss the implication of these changes for
  producers, disseminators and users of public data. * Get a hands-on
  look at finding aids and access tools at the Agency Showcase and at
  daily vendor displays.

     In addition to the traditional APDU literature table, this year's
     conference will feature member displays. The displays will be
     available for viewing during all break periods.  Make it a point
     to check on what's new and interesting from members.
