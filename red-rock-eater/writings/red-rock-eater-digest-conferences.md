I realize not everyone is interested in the conference announcements
I send out.  After all, most people will never attend any of them.
But hey, I won't attend more than a vanishing proportion of them
either.  I find them fascinating for several reasons.  They remind me
how big the world is, and how small.  They give me a sense of knowing
what everyone in the research world and other relevant institutions
is doing, or at least what they publicly say they're doing and what
language they use to say it.  Fortunately, numerous RRE readers share
my fascination, and several of them have self-organized to assemble
the enclosed absurdly long list of upcoming conferences that relate
to the social-aspects-of-computing types of topics that RRE covers.
The conferences are loosely grouped into six categories: information
systems in context, information society, interface, economics and
commerce, libraries and museums, and the incredibly huge world of
education technology.  We thought of assembling a humorous list of
the odd conferences that also get organized in the world (e.g., the
"Reciprocal Meat Conference") but we decided it would be too easy.
