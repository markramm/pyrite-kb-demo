Connections 98 Conference
Call for Papers

The University of Western Ontario, Faculty of Information and Media
Studies, is hosting the fourth annual Connections conference in London,
Ontario, Canada, on October 24th and 25th, 1998.  This conference will
provide a forum for the exchange of research among doctoral students
from all library and information science related fields.  Possible
topics for conference papers include, but are not limited to:

* * abstracting and indexing* * archival theory and practice* * digital libraries* * electronic documents* * history of publishing, authorship, and reading* * human-computer interaction* * imaging and multimedia* * information management* * information needs and uses* * information policy* * information retrieval* * information systems and architecture* * Internet and World Wide Web* * knowledge management* * management of information in organizations* * media and communication studies* * museum studies

Abstracts submissions will be accepted from currently enrolled doctoral
students.  Reports of research in progress as well as recently completed
studies are welcome.  Students engaged in the early phases of doctoral
work are encouraged to submit abstracts which explore theoretical or
other elements related to their proposed dissertation topics.  Please
note that presentations accepted for the conference will be limited to
15 minutes, followed by a 5 minute discussion period.  Abstracts which
have been accepted will be printed in a proceedings volume to be
distributed at the conference, and will be posted on the Connections 98
web site.

Please submit an abstract of no more than 300 words by July 31, 1998.
All abstracts submitted will be blind refereed by doctoral students and
faculty at the host institution.  Presenters will be notified of their
status by September 4, 1998.

Both hard copy and electronic submissions will be accepted.  Those
submitting abstracts in print form are asked to provide three (3)
copies.  Electronic submissions must be submitted as part of the text of
an electronic mail message, or in ASCII format.

Be sure to include the following information with all submissions:

* * your name* * address* * telephone number* * email address* * institutional affiliation
