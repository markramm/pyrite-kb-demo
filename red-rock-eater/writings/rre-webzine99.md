WEBZINE99 -------------------------------------------------------
AN EXHIBITION, FORUM, AND PARTY FOR INDEPENDENT PUBLISHING ON THE
INTERNET.  CELEBRATE, DEMYSTIFY, AND EMPOWER WITH A WEBZINE-MAKING
WORKSHOP, PERFORMANCES, INTERACTIVE INSTALLATIONS, AND SPEAKERS
AND PANELISTS.

San Francisco, CA -- On July 24th, 1999, WEBZINE99 will celebrate the
unwavering efforts of brave pioneers of independent creativity and
provide a learning space where artists, writers, and programmers can
participate in open communion about the current state of independent
web publishing.

Featured keynote speakers are Phil Agre, associate professor of
information studies at UCLA and editor of Red Rock Eater News Service,
Larry Harvey, founder of the Burning Man Project, and V. Vale, founder
of Search & Destroy and RE/search Publications.  Also, Mayor Willie
Brown of San Francisco, at a special address from the podium, will
proclaim July 24th as "Internet Independence Day".

"I'm tired of the over-hyped buzz about venture capitalists and
e-commerce startups." says event coordinator Ryan Junell.  "The web
is and can be *so much more than that.  What we need as a culture is
to get together and share what we know about this medium.  I'm hoping
that WEBZINE99 will generate some semblance of meaning now while this
global medium is young."

"Independent media is what will save our asses from boredom.  There
is so much creative energy out there that isn't wrapped around a
shopping cart or a search engine." says Eddie Codel, event manipulator.
"This conference will hit you upside your head with compelling and
revolutionary digital creations that have yet to be exploited on
E-Bay."

The daylong event features speakers and panelists from many of
the web's best underground productions.  Workshops and on-site
installations offer attendees the opportunity to participate in
the creative process firsthand.

Tickets are only $7 if purchased online in advance
http://www.tixtogo.com and $5 - $10 sliding scale at the door.
Attendees under age 18 will be admitted free of charge.  The event
runs from 2pm to 2am on Saturday, July 24th.  The venue is SOMARTS
Cultural Center, 934 Brannan (b/w 8th and 9th) in San Francisco.
Food and drinks will be available.  Everyone welcome.
