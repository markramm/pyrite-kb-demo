In this 14th issue of BHSUG, Biometrics were very, very big at
CardTech SecurTech '99 in Chicago last week.  There was almost too
much to take in.  Your BHSUG editor was there and came back with some
of the seminar program highlights.  NIST and Texas are both making a
move on finger print templates.  Jim Wayman gives us some points to
ponder on this possible move toward standards.  The state of Texas may
just lead the way and give us a 'user' standard long before the smoke
clears from other industry discussion groups.  Also in the Human
Services arena, there are state project updates from Connecticut and
Illinois.  Barry Beckwith gives us a run down on the recent Illinois
pilots of both retinal scanning and finger imaging.  The IBG completes
a biometric evaluation for CitiBank and Visa, and the IBIA announces
their privacy principles.  This issue of BHSUG has the latest
international "Biometric Events" calendar as well as our cornerstone
"Biometric Watch", containing news briefs from all over the Biometric
world.
