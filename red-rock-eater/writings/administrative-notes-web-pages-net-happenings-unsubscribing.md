# administrative notes -- web pages, net-happenings, unsubscribing

```
I'm sorry that you're getting so many of these administrative notes.

I've just found out (or had somehow forgotten) that RRE is available on the
WorldWide Web in two separate places.  Al Whaley <al.whaley@sunnyside.com>,
who runs the most excellent CPSR gopher and web pages (gopher.cpsr.org,
http://www.cpsr.org/home), has past RRE messages listed alphabetically 
by subject line at http://www.cpsr.org/cpsr/lists/rre and Kee Hinckley
<nazgul@utopia.com> (see also http://www.utopia.com/infocenter.shtml) has 
them listed chronologically at http://www.utopia.com/mailings/rre/

I'm sorry for my multiple slip-ups in announcing the web pages for The Network
Observer.  In case any confusion remains, the correct URL is as follows:
http://communication.ucsd.edu/pagre/tno.html

Also, "Networking on the Network" is now available on WWW.  The URL is:
http://communication.ucsd.edu/pagre/network.html

Long-time RRE subscribers might have noticed that I have not been filtering
the very high-volume net-happenings mailing list (which is run by Gleason
Sackman <sackman@plains.nodak.edu>) since springtime.  I took myself off of
net-happenings in June to get some rest over the summer, and I've decided
not to rejoin it because filtering it is a great deal of work.  I know that
quite a few RRE subscribers do read net-happenings, though, and any of them
would be quite welcome to pass along items from net-happenings that they think
might be good for RRE.  Let me thank the people who are already sending me a
steady trickle of good stuff from one source or another.

As usual, let me remind you that you can unsubscribe from RRE like so:

  To: rre-request@weber.ucsd.edu
  Subject: unsubscribe

Tell your friends about RRE.

Thanks a lot.

Phil
```
