                              CitySpace Project

*******************************************************************************

                          Come Build it Yourself.

        CitySpace is a virtual city model built by kids across the
internet. The model is made up of stories, pictures, sounds, and 3D objects
from kids' neighborhoods and imaginary spaces worldwide.  CitySpace is a
place to connect with other kids, to share each other's work, and to
experiment with emerging digital communications tools.  The model exists as
an onsite exhibit at the Exploratorium, and as a shared environment across
the internet.

        During the week of March 7-13, individuals and groups of kids from
across the network are invited to join in the ongoing construction of the
CitySpace model. What to send? Stories about your neighborhood, scanned
photographs, hand drawn pictures, audio samples, 3d models- anything you
can digitize and anything you can imagine. Working closely with a team of
kids, artists, and developers at the Exploratorium, these submisions will
become part of the evolving model, shared by each of its builders.

        The model will become an interactive exhibit as part of the
Exploratorium's Multimedia Playground Exhibition, and demonstrations will
be given throughout the week. For distant CitySpace builders and others
interested in exploring the collaborative results of the model,  live video
connections can be made to CitySpace using public domain CU-SeeMe software.
For those unable to receive transmissions using CU-SeeMe,  still images and
short digital video clips will be made publically available on the network.
 

        For more information on how to participate, mail to
info@cityspace.org, or using the web, connect to www.exploratorium.edu and
look under "Current Exploratorium Events". CU-SeeMe was developed at
Cornell is available via anonymous FTP from gated.cornell.edu in the
pub/video directory.

*******************************************************************************
  CitySpace at the Exploratorium has been made possible by USENIX and SGI Inc.
*******************************************************************************
