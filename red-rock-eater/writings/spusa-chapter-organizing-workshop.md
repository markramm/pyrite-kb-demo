# SPUSA Chapter Organizing Workshop

```
Date: Wed, 24 May 1995 08:00:15 -0700
From: Student Pugwash USA <uspugwash@igc.apc.org>
Subject: SPUSA Chapter Organizing Workshop

Student Pugwash USA's 
1995 CHAPTER ORGANIZING WORKSHOP:
LEADERSHIP FOR A SUSTAINABLE FUTURE

Student Pugwash USA is a national, educational, 
non-profit organization dedicated to building a 
commitment among young people to solve critical 
global problems through the responsible use of 
science and technology.  Through interactive, 
international and interdisciplinary programs, 
Student Pugwash USA educates young people on the 
relevance of science and technology in their own 
lives, and on its ability to shape the future of 
the global community.

Student Pugwash USA's 1995 CHAPTER ORGANIZING WORKSHOP:  
LEADERSHIP FOR A SUSTAINABLE FUTURE  offers students 
from across the country the opportunity to develop the 
skills to educate both themselves and others, and 
increase awareness and activism on the critical issues 
at the juncture of science and technology such as energy 
and environment, peace and security, science education, 
health and bioethics, and population and development.  
Students participate in specially designed workshops 
to develop innovative approaches to leadership skills, 
event planning, brainstorming techniques, and motivational 
tools that help make campus organizing hassle free, and a 
fun way to spend "spare" time.

If you are a faculty member, we would greatly appreciate 
it if you could spread the word by informing your students 
of this opportunity.  If you are a student, we encourage 
you to apply.

The Chapter Organizing Workshop will be held JULY 27-30, 1995 
in CHEVY CHASE, MD (near Washington, DC).  The application 
DEADLINE IS JUNE 2, 1995, though late applications will be 
accepted with a $10 late fee.  The cost of the weekend 
is $55 and INCLUDES ALL FOOD AND LODGING.  Some travel 
assistance is available.

Should you have any questions, please do not hesitate 
to call Beth McKay, National Chapter Coordinator, at 
uspugwash@igc.org, or at our toll-free number for 
students, 1-800-WOW-A-PUG.
```
