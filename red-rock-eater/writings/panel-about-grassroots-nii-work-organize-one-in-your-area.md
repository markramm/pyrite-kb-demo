Date: Thu, 10 Nov 1994 10:47:06 EST
From: hkklein@MIT.EDU
Subject: TPR-NE:  Up-Coming Forum on Grassroots NII

For people in the Boston area, this forum is an opportunity to learn
about issues and activism bringing groups together in TPR-Northeast.  
For people elsewhere in the country, this kind of forum is interesting, 
easy to organize, and promotes grassroots participation.  

-- Hans Klein
CPSR/Boston

          TELEDEMOCRACY and UNIVERSAL SERVICE
 
      Info Superhighway Priorities and Public Participation in the
                             Policymaking Process.
 
              First in series of public forums on
     Directions for the National Information Infrastructure.
 
   Sponsored by The Telecommunications Policy Roundtable N. E.
                     Wednesday, November 16, 1994, 7-9pm
               Emerson College Student Union
                96 Beacon St, Boston, Mass.
 
How will decisions be made regarding the direction of the 
communications infrastructure for the next century?
What will the priorities be for establishing this new network?
Should government take a hands-off position and let the market 
determine the direction?  Or should we insure that all citizens 
have some minimum access to the tools of the next 
communications revolution? 
 
To address these questions the Telecommunications Policy 
Roundtable N.E. has assembled a panel reflecting a broad range of 
opinion on the issue of public participation in the development of the 
National Information Infrastructure.  The panel will include 
representatives of the telecommunications industry, the 
community computers movement, and non profit educational and 
public interest organizations, as well as specialists in the 
design of electronic networks that assist in community building and
citizen participation in public policy-making.  Panelists include:
 
Stan Kugell, Vice President, Pilgrim Telephone, Inc.
Miles Fidelman, Director, Center For Civic Networking
Eric Loeb, Network designer, Artificial Inteligence Lab, MIT
Howard Frederick, Professor of Mass Communications, Emerson 
Kate Snow, Director, Somerville Community Computing Center
Allen Shaw, Developer of Community Networks, Media Lab, MIT
 
For more information, contact the Telecommunications Policy 
Roundtable N.E.  through:
Paul Johnson                             Coralee Whitcomb
pjbrady@delphi.com                      cwhitcom@bentley.edu
phone (508) 653-5987
 
Emerson Student Union is at 96 Beacon St., Boston, on the 
northwest corner of Boston Public Garden--4 blocks north of the 
Arlington stop on the Green Line.
