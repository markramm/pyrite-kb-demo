The Summer Webcastings of De HoeksteenNet.

From July 5th 1999 on De HoeksteenNet will webcast a series of
programs from the headquarters of the DDS (the Digital city in
Amsterdam) Titled "the Summer webcastings of De HoeksteenNet"

After six years of local television programming for Salto (Amsterdam
local television) De Hoeksteen Communications concentrates now
in international programing via real video in the world wide web.
he main goal of this new project is not only to program for an
international viewer ship but also to incorporate users and make them
active participants of these series by means of point - to - point and
multipoint video conferencing, moderated discussion groups, IRC, ICQ
and e-mail as well - of course- as telephone and fax.

All programming of De Hoeksteen Live! Television was webcasted from
1994 until the very last program in April 1998 but this is the first
time that all energy and effort will be concentrated in streaming
media and the interaction with IRC, e-mail and all the rest.

Programming includes politics, finances, international relations,
culture, communications and current affairs.  Interviews, discussions
commentaries and round tables will be conducted in Dutch, English,
Spanish, Italian, German and French at first but one of the main,
long term objectives is to enlarge the number of languages in the
best possible way.

Theater, music and the visual arts are an integral part of the
programming and groups and individuals are encouraged to participate
and to incorporate the interactive facilities available from the DDS
in their contributions to the webcastings.

All programming is transmitted in two different channels one for users
with ISDN, ADSL, T1 and Cable Modem and a second one for users with
regular telephone lines.

The Summer Webcastings of De HoeksteenNet is a co-operation of De
Hoeksteen and the DDS in Amsterdam

July the Fifth

The July Fifth webcasting of De HoeksteenNet will take place from
16.00 until 19.00 C.E.T. (Central European Time) and it can be found
in the web page of De HoeksteenNet http://www.Desk.nl/~hksteen/

This first program includes interviews with Harry van Bommel MP and
Defense Spokesman for the SP (Dutch -extreme left- Socialist Party)
The interview will be conducted by Colombian journalist Alfonso
Montealegre who has been living in the Netherlands for almost 30
years and it deals with the position of the SP on the role of Nato
as a peace keeping force and the role of The Netherlands within Nato.
Harry van Bommel wrote and article on the subject for "Militaire-
Spectator" titled "Koersen naar een andere defensie" (courses
towards a different defense policy) With The Summer Webcastings of
De HoeksteenNet a new permanent subject is included in programming
on a permanent basis "Amsterdam Queer Desk" headed by Mattias Duyves.
In this first issue he will interview Australian activist living in
Amsterdam Richard Keldoules.

Another high light of the program is an interview with Amsterdam
council member Ton Hooijmaijers from the VVD (Dutch biggest liberal,
market oriented party) on the ROA the Greater Amsterdam Board
( G. A. B.)  as a pilot for the come back of the City State concept in
the European Union.  The interview is conducted by Colombian political
scientist and book editor Daniel Ramos.

Dutch stage actor and director Titus Muizelaar will read fragments
of a play especially written by British playwright Harold Pinter for
Muizelaar.  Amsterdam city council member and chairman of the media
and communications commission Ab Cherribi (Labour) opens a series of
political commentaries in French.

Hoeksteen Senior Anchorman Martin Bosma is the Master of Ceremonies
for the entire series and Rob Zwetsloot "the face of Amsterdam Cable"
is there with commentaries and musical entertainment.

A special appearance by German Artist Ulay is the final touch for this
first webcasting of the Summer Series.

Dutch Economist and Hoeksteen veteran Erik Bartelsman will moderate
the discussion group while Juan Pablo Jimenez Marroquin and Lucas
Marroquin Olivera will be encharged of IRC and ICQ from Amsterdam and
Bogota respectively.

The program is produced by Dutch media artist and former television
producer of De Hoeksteen Live! Television, MauzZ.  Digital
communications are under the supervision of Guido Vriesinga.

Zoe D'Amaro and Daniella Savellini coordinate the editorial desk.
Idzarda Linderbergh is the executive producer of the series.  A Second
webcasting is already scheduled for Saturday July the 17th featuring
Jan Hoekma MP member of the Defense Commission and chairman of the
Atlantic Commission of the Lower House of the Dutch Parliament and
American composer Philip Glass, playing live from his studio in New
York City.

Other important guest in this occasion are Eduardo Valencia Ospina
Register / Secretary General of the International Court of Justice
in The Hague and British media artist David Gracia head of the
interactive media department of the HKU in Hilversum.

Future webcastings include Marjet van Zuijlen MP as head of De
Hoeksteen Media Desk, Oussama Cherribi MP as contributing editor
Dick van Eyk director of the Internet division of the NRC Handelsblad
and Roel Janssen Financial Editor of the same daily, as well as guest
appearances by Hans Hoogervorst Secretary of State for Social Affairs
of the Netherlands, Roberto Payer Director of the Hilton Hotel in
Amsterdam and chairman of the Hilton Holding Benelux, Prof. Dr. Rick
van der Ploeg Secretary of State for Culture of the Netherlands and
many others.

De Hoeksteen is grateful to Lisbeth van de Kar from the DDS for her
support and co-operation in the realization of this series.
