The press reports that if the Bush campaign fails in its attempt to
suppress the hand recounts of voters' ballots that are provided by
Florida law, as seems likely, then they will implement a "scorched-
earth" plan involving legal challenges to the election outcomes in
Iowa, New Mexico, Oregon, and Wisconsin.

For the situation in Florida see:

  Palm Beach Orders Manual Recount; Gore Closes Gap
  http://www.nytimes.com/aponline/politics/12WIRE-PALM.html

For the Bush plan see:

  Bush Team Prepares "Scorched-Earth" Plan
  http://washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A2830-2000Nov11.html

  Bush considers recounts in Iowa, Wisconsin
  http://www.salon.com/politics/wire/2000/11/11/bush_recounts/

For the political side see:

  Bush Signed Recount Rule in Texas
  http://www.nytimes.com/aponline/politics/AP-ELN-Bush-Texas-Recounts.html

  Has Bush Blown It in Florida?
  http://slate.msn.com/code/kausfiles/kausfiles.asp?idMessage=6473

  Florida's Volusia Triangle
  http://www.salon.com/politics/feature/2000/11/11/volusia/

  Tragicomedy of Errors Fuels Volusia Recount
  http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A2748-2000Nov11.html

If the envisioned scorched-earth plan does come to pass then it will
be important to monitor the regional newspapers in Iowa, New Mexico,
Oregon, and Wisconsin.  To that end, some very excellent RRE readers
have assembled guides to the regional newspapers and other relevant
online resources in those states.  I have enclosed them here: two for
Iowa, followed by one each for the other states.  I have edited and
reformatted them, but they are not my work.  Nor have I tested the
URL's myself.  I assume they're fine.

Let me just add that one of my favorite words in the whole English
language is "slugfest".  It's gotten a lot of exercise this week.
