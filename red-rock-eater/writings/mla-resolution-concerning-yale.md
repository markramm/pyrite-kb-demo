# MLA Resolution Concerning Yale

```
Date: Wed Jan 3 11:10:22 PST 1996
From: nagps@netcom.com
To: nagps-official@nagps.varesearch.com
Subject: MLA Resolution Concerning Yale

Dear NAGPS Members and Friends:

NAGPS received a copy of the Modern Language Association resolution 
regarding the activities of teaching assistants at Yale University.

The source of the text is listed at the bottom of the message.

Kevin Boyer
NAGPS Executive Director
NAGPS@NETCOM.COM

###################################

Whereas the Modern Language Association, as an organization of scholars
and teachers of language and literature, dedicated to the maintenance of
professional standards and intellectual freedom, registers its deep
concern regarding the academic reprisals that Yale University is taking
against graduate student teachers participating in union activity; and

Whereas the Modern Language Association further recognizes that, whatever
opinion its individual members may have about the advisability of union
organizing among graduate students members of the teaching staff at Yale,
or the advisability of the current grade strike at Yale, there is no
question that participation in such activities without fear of
retribution designed to  injure one's academic career is a fundamental
aspect of academic freedom; and

Whereas the Modern Language Association recognizes that the recent
actions of the Yale administration which inappropriately encourage the
use of union activity as a criterion for academic evaluation and which
seek to punish union activity through academic reprisals, including
witheld letters of recommendation, disciplinary letters, academic
probation, firing of teachers, denial of promised teaching jobs, or
expulsion, constitute a significant violation of academic freedom; and

Whereas the Modern Language Association further recognizes that the
independence of faculty must be protected, especially in situations of
conflict such as that which has arisen between graduate students and
administrators at Yale; and calls on the Yale administration to respect
the independence of faculty in the current strike and to refrain from any
attempts to enroll faculty members to serve as replacement workers for
striking graduate student teaching staff or to enforce administrative
discipline on strike participants; and

Whereas it is incumbent on university administrations to guarantee that
all members of a campus community are free to pursue political
activities, including union activities, as an exercise of free speech
without fear of academic reprisal, and the Modern Language Association is
concerned that Yale President Richard Levin has refused requests from
over 300 faculty members (at Yale and elsewhere) to establish a policy
prohibiting the use of union activity as a criterion in academic
evaluation;

Be it resolved that the Modern Language Association hereby records its
censure of the Yale University Administration for permitting the use of
union activity as a criterion for academic evaluations, and for failing
to protect the right of Yale's graduate teaching staff to participate in
union activities, including job actions, without fear of reprisals
against their academic careers.

---

Victoria Smallman, Department of English, McMaster University
e-mail: g9126311@mcmail.cis.mcmaster.ca
```
