The Donella Meadows Institute is the nonprofit organization that carries forward Donella Meadows's work following her death in February 2001. It is the renamed successor to the [[sustainability-institute]] that Meadows founded in 1996 in Hartland, Vermont. The renaming honored her legacy while maintaining the organizational continuity she had built.

[[diana-wright]], who had been a key staff member at the Sustainability Institute, continued working through the Donella Meadows Institute and was the primary figure responsible for completing [[thinking-in-systems-2008]] from Meadows's draft manuscript. The institute's most consequential act in the [[posthumous-influence-2001-present]] era was enabling that book's publication by [[chelsea-green-publishing]] in 2008 — seven years after Meadows's death.

The institute's programs include the "Leverage Points" leadership development program (named for [[leverage-points-paper-1999]]), educational resources based on [[thinking-in-systems-2008]], and curriculum development for systems thinking in K-12 and higher education. These programs represent the applied pedagogical mission that Meadows pursued throughout the [[dartmouth-and-global-citizen-1972-2001]] era, continued institutionally after her death.

The institute also maintains and disseminates the [[global-citizen-columns]], Meadows's archived newspaper writing, which now functions as an educational resource demonstrating how systems thinking applies to everyday policy and social issues. The columns — written over sixteen years from 1985 to 2001 — are accessible to general audiences and serve as entry points to Meadows's ideas for readers who encounter [[thinking-in-systems-2008]] as their first contact.

The [[balaton-group]], which Meadows convened annually for nearly two decades, continues under the institute's organizational umbrella, sustaining the international network of systems thinkers and sustainability researchers she built during the [[balaton-and-sustainability-indicators-1982-2001]] era.

The institute's location in the same Vermont community as [[dartmouth-college]] and [[chelsea-green-publishing]] maintains the regional ecosystem of sustainability-oriented institutions that characterized Meadows's working life. Its continued existence ensures that the [[sustainability-institute]]'s applied research agenda — [[indicators-of-sustainability-1998]], the [[state-of-the-village-report]], and the broader sustainability indicators work — remains an active program rather than a historical archive.
