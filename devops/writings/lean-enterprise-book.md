Won the Shingo Publication Award. [[jez-humble]] with Joanne Molesky and Barry O'Reilly. Extends the lean and DevOps principles from [[continuous-delivery-book]] to the enterprise level — specifically addressing the question of how large organizations can innovate with the agility of startups.

## The Problem Addressed

By 2015, continuous delivery was well-established in technology-native companies (Etsy, Netflix, Amazon, Google). The remaining challenge was enterprise adoption: large organizations with regulatory constraints, complex legacy systems, and governance structures designed for waterfall delivery.

Lean Enterprise argues that the tools of lean startup (hypothesis-driven development, validated learning, build-measure-learn) apply at enterprise scale when combined with the delivery infrastructure of [[continuous-delivery-book]] and the organizational practices of lean manufacturing.

## Key Contributions

**Lean Startup in the Enterprise**: Eric Ries's lean startup principles (minimum viable products, pivot or persevere decisions, validated learning) translated to enterprise contexts where teams must work within governance structures and alongside existing products.

**Continuous Delivery at Scale**: Architectural and organizational patterns for applying [[continuous-delivery-book]] practices in large organizations with many teams. Microservices, platform teams, and independently deployable services as enablers of enterprise-scale delivery.

**Organizational Learning**: Drawing on Peter Senge's learning organization concepts (see Senge KB), [[jez-humble]] frames continuous improvement as requiring not just technical practices but organizational structures that enable experimentation and learning from failure.

## Relationship to Accelerate

[[accelerate-book]] (2018) validates several of Lean Enterprise's prescriptions empirically — particularly the importance of loosely coupled architecture and the capability of teams to deploy independently. The Lean Enterprise prescriptions were evidence-based practitioner intuition; Accelerate turned them into causal findings.

## Positioning

Lean Enterprise occupies a space between the practitioner handbooks ([[the-devops-handbook]]) and the executive-facing novels ([[the-phoenix-project]]). It is more technically grounded than the novels and more organizationally focused than [[continuous-delivery-book]]. The Shingo Award reflects its systematic engagement with lean manufacturing principles.
