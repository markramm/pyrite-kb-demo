Tom Poppendieck is co-author with [[mary-poppendieck]] of the lean software development book series. With a background in physics and IT systems, Tom contributed analytical rigor to the Poppendiecks' translation of lean principles into software development.

His analytical orientation aligned with Reinertsen's mathematically grounded approach. The Poppendiecks' books draw on Reinertsen's [[economic-framework-for-prioritization]] and [[cost-of-delay]] as core justifications for lean practices — the argument that lean is not just about process efficiency but about economic optimization of value flow. Tom's systems thinking background helped ground the economic reasoning that Reinertsen had developed in the product development context and apply it to software organizations.
