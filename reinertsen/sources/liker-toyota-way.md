Liker's comprehensive account of Toyota's management principles. Provides important context for understanding what Reinertsen builds on and critiques. Reinertsen's "second generation lean" argument in [[principles-of-product-development-flow]] is partly a response to practitioners who mechanically applied Toyota manufacturing practices to product development without the [[economic-framework-for-prioritization]] that Reinertsen argues is essential. [[taiichi-ohno]]'s system, as documented by Liker, is the manufacturing foundation Reinertsen both builds on and transcends.
