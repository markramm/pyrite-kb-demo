# The Progression: Uncertainty to Chaos

## Boyd's Causal Chain
Boyd described the collapse of moral cohesion as a deterministic progression, not a random failure:

**Uncertainty → Doubt → Mistrust → Confusion → Disorder → Fear → Panic → Chaos**

This is not poetry. It is a causal chain. Each psychological state produces the next. The progression runs from the mildest disruption toward total collapse.

## How It Works
- **Uncertainty**: The official frame doesn't perfectly fit observable reality. Members notice discrepancies but haven't yet formed conclusions.
- **Doubt**: Repeated mismatch produces active questioning. The frame is no longer automatically trusted.
- **Mistrust**: Doubt extends to the people and institutions maintaining the frame. Not just 'the narrative is wrong' but 'the people telling us this are lying.'
- **Confusion**: Without a trusted frame, members cannot make sense of incoming information. Decision-making degrades.
- **Disorder**: Confused members act incoherently. Coordination breaks down. Actions contradict each other.
- **Fear**: Disorder without resolution produces fear. The coalition cannot predict what will happen next, including to its own members.
- **Panic**: Fear accelerates into reactive, uncoordinated action. The progression self-amplifies.
- **Chaos**: Total collapse of coordinated function.

## The Weapon and the Defense
The progression can be weaponized (FUD: inject uncertainty, accelerate to doubt and mistrust) or defended against (maintain harmony and a grand ideal that holds the progression at uncertainty).

A coalition with strong moral cohesion — harmony, shared grand ideal, moral authority — absorbs uncertainty without it metastasizing into doubt. The frame holds. The progression stalls.

A coalition with weak moral cohesion — collapsing narrative, exposed hypocrisy, defecting allies — cannot hold at uncertainty. Each exposure accelerates the progression.

## The FUD Wars Application
Microsoft's strategy against Linux was a textbook application of this progression:
- Inject **uncertainty**: 'Will Linux scale for enterprise?'
- Breed **doubt**: 'Is open source legally safe? What about IP contamination?'
- Create **mistrust**: 'Who is really behind this? Is it anti-American?'

The goal was to hold enterprise buyers at doubt and mistrust — never letting them reach the confirmation that would collapse the FUD.

It failed because the open source coalition had a grand ideal that matched observable reality. Developers could see the code. The licenses were clear. The community was real. The FUD couldn't create sustained mismatch because the truth was verifiable.

## Minneapolis 2026
The administration attempted to run this progression against the Minneapolis coalition:
- Inject uncertainty: 'Are the protesters really peaceful?'
- Breed doubt: 'Could some of them be dangerous?'
- Create mistrust: 'Are outside agitators involved?'

Simultaneously, the documentation infrastructure ran the progression inside the administration's coalition:
- Uncertainty: 'The videos don't quite match our claims'
- Doubt: 'Maybe we can't control this narrative'
- Mistrust: 'Our own allies are questioning us'
- Confusion: 'We can't maintain a coherent story'
- Disorder: 'The commander was removed'

Minneapolis held at uncertainty. The administration traveled toward disorder.
