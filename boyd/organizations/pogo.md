The Project On Government Oversight (originally the Project on Military Procurement) was founded in 1981 as a nonpartisan watchdog organization focused on defense spending and government accountability. POGO became a key institutional home for the [[strategist-and-reformer|Military Reform Movement's]] civilian wing. [[sprey-pierre|Pierre Sprey]], Boyd's Fighter Mafia co-founder, was closely associated with POGO's defense work. The organization provided an institutional platform for reform ideas that the Pentagon's internal culture suppressed, and continued the reformist mission after [[john-boyd|Boyd's]] death. POGO represents the institutional legacy of Boyd's "To Be or To Do" ethic — an organization built around the principle that accountability matters more than institutional comfort.
