The Office of the Director of Operational Test and Evaluation (DOT&E) was created by Congress in 1983 to provide independent testing of weapons systems before full-scale production. [[christie-thomas|Thomas Christie]], [[john-boyd|Boyd's]] collaborator on [[energy-maneuverability-theory|E-M theory]], served as the first Director of Operational Test and Evaluation. The office represented an institutional embodiment of Boyd's reformist principles: independent evaluation, empirical testing, and resistance to institutional pressure to approve systems prematurely. DOT&E became a key node in the [[strategist-and-reformer|Military Reform Movement's]] effort to hold the Pentagon accountable for weapons system performance.
