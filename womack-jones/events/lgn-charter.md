In September 2007, [[james-p-womack]], [[daniel-t-jones]], and [[jose-ferro]] formally chartered the [[lean-global-network]] (LGN), creating an international consortium of nonprofit Lean institutes. The chartering of LGN is the culmination of the institution-building phase of the Womack-Jones project, extending the dissemination infrastructure beyond the two anchor institutes — [[lean-enterprise-institute]] (US) and [[lean-enterprise-academy]] (UK) — into a federated global network.

## Significance

The LGN chartering represents the **global scaling** of the Lean transmission chain. Where the research phase ([[imvp-launch]], 1985) produced the empirical foundation and the codification phase ([[machine-publication]], 1990; [[lean-thinking-publication]], 1996) produced the framework, the institution-building phase culminated in a network capable of sustaining, localizing, and extending Lean thinking in 30+ countries without requiring direct involvement from Womack and Jones.

The inclusion of [[jose-ferro]] as a co-founding principal reflects the international scope of the project — Ferro's institute (believed to be the Lean Institute Brasil, though unverified) brought a Latin American anchor to the network alongside the US and UK founders.

## Network Architecture

LGN operates as a consortium of independent nonprofit institutes, each responsible for Lean education and practitioner development in their geography. The federated structure allows for local cultural adaptation while maintaining shared intellectual foundations in [[five-lean-principles]] and the broader [[lean-enterprise]] framework.
