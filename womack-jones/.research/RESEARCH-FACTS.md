# Womack & Jones KB — Verified Facts Sheet

Working document for Phase 2. Not a KB entry. Sources noted for each fact.

## James P. Womack — Biography

- Born: October 30, 1948, New Orleans, Louisiana (source: Prabook)
- BA Political Science, University of Chicago, 1970 (source: LEI bio, Prabook)
- MA Transportation Systems, Harvard University, 1975 (source: LEI bio, Prabook)
- PhD Political Science, MIT, 1982 — dissertation on comparative industrial policy in US, Germany, Japan (source: LEI bio, Prabook)
- MIT research scientist, 1975-1991 (source: Amazon author page, LEI bio)
- Led IMVP at MIT in the late 1980s (source: LEI bio)
- Founded Lean Enterprise Institute, 1997 (source: LEI bio)
- Chairman and CEO of LEI until 2010 (source: QAD blog)
- Senior Advisor at LEI, 2010-present (source: LEI bio)

## Daniel T. Jones — Biography

- Birth date: not confirmed in sources
- BA Economics, University of Sussex (source: LEI bio)
- European Director of MIT's Future of the Automobile and IMVP programs (source: LEI bio)
- Founded Lean Enterprise Academy, UK in 2003 (source: LEI bio)
- Senior Advisor to LEI (source: LEI bio)
- Advised UK government on Rethinking Construction report (source: LEI bio)
- Helped establish first company University in UK at Unipart (source: LEI bio)
- Organized Lean Summit conferences in Europe (source: LEI bio)

## Daniel Roos — Biography

- Born: April 12, 1939, Brooklyn, NY (source: Prabook)
- SB 1961, SM 1963, PhD 1966, all MIT Civil Engineering (source: MIT IDSS page, Prabook)
- MIT faculty since 1966; Professor since 1976 (source: MIT pages)
- Japan Steel Industry Professor since 1985 (source: Prabook)
- Founding Director, IMVP (source: MIT pages)
- Founding Director, MIT Engineering Systems Division, 1998-2004 (source: MIT pages)
- Director, MIT Center for Transportation Studies (source: MIT pages)
- Shingo Prize for Excellence in Manufacturing Research, 1994 (source: MIT pages)

## John Krafcik

- Coined the term "lean production" in 1988 article "Triumph of the Lean Production System" (source: Creative Safety Supply, Lean Blog)
- Published in MIT Sloan Management Review, Fall 1988 (source: multiple)
- Based on his master's thesis at MIT Sloan School of Management (source: multiple)
- Studied under James P. Womack (source: Creative Safety Supply)
- IMVP researcher, 1986-1990 (source: multiple)
- Later career: CEO of Hyundai Motor America, then CEO of Waymo (source: Wikipedia search results)

## Publications — Verified Details

### The Machine That Changed the World (1990)
- Authors: James P. Womack, Daniel T. Jones, Daniel Roos
- Also credits Donna Sammons Carpenter (source: Wikipedia search snippet)
- Publisher: Macmillan/Rawson Associates, 1990
- Revised edition: 2007
- Result of 5-year, $5 million MIT IMVP study
- Published in 11 languages, sold over 600,000 copies (source: MIT Roos page)
- Shingo Prize winner
- Subtitle: "The Story of Lean Production—Toyota's Secret Weapon in the Global Car Wars That Is Now Revolutionizing World Industry" (source: Amazon)

### Lean Thinking (1996)
- Authors: James P. Womack, Daniel T. Jones
- Publisher: Simon & Schuster, 1996
- Revised and Updated edition: 2003 (source: Amazon ISBN 0743249275)
- Subtitle: "Banish Waste and Create Wealth in Your Corporation"
- Codifies the five lean principles: Value, Value Stream, Flow, Pull, Perfection
- Shingo Prize winner

### Lean Solutions (2005)
- Authors: James P. Womack, Daniel T. Jones
- Publisher: Simon & Schuster, 2005
- Subtitle: "How Companies and Customers Can Create Value and Wealth Together"
- Introduces "lean consumption" concept

### Seeing the Whole (2002)
- Authors: Daniel T. Jones, James P. Womack
- Publisher: Lean Enterprise Institute, 2002
- Full title: "Seeing the Whole: Mapping the Extended Value Stream"
- Workbook on extended value stream mapping
- Second edition published later with additional examples
- Shingo Prize winner

### Gemba Walks (2011/2013)
- Author: James P. Womack (solo)
- Publisher: Lean Enterprise Institute
- First edition: 2011 (approximate — some sources say 2011, others 2013)
- Second edition: 2013 with 12 new essays
- Collection of 60+ essays from Womack's gemba walks worldwide

## Organizations — Verified

### Lean Enterprise Institute (LEI)
- Founded: 1997 by James P. Womack
- Location: Cambridge, MA (later Brookline, MA)
- Type: nonprofit education, publishing, research, and conference organization
- Womack was Chairman/CEO until 2010, then Senior Advisor

### Lean Enterprise Academy (LEA)
- Founded by Daniel T. Jones
- Location: UK
- Founding date: 2003

### Lean Global Network (LGN)
- Founded: September 2007
- Founded by: Jim Womack and Dan Jones
- Formally chartered September 2007
- 30+ member institutes worldwide
- Jose Ferro (Brazil) was third founding leader alongside Womack and Jones

### MIT International Motor Vehicle Program (IMVP)
- Multi-year, $5 million+ research program at MIT
- Daniel Roos: founding director
- Now called Program on Vehicle and Mobility Innovation (PVMI), based at UPenn
- Key researchers: Womack, Jones, Krafcik, Takahiro Fujimoto, John Paul MacDuffie
